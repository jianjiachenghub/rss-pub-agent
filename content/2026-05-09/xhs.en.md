Today's top must-see tech roundup 🔥

First up, the biggest AI news of the day:

🟢 **EMO Model**: A new twist on Mixture of Experts!
Simply put, it’s about letting AI automatically learn to "specialize" during training—some experts focus on medical knowledge, others on code. In practice, only a small subset of experts (e.g., 12.5%) needs to be activated to complete tasks, yet performance stays close to using the full model! This could make deploying large models cheaper and more flexible in the future.

🟢 **Ant Group's Big Move**: Trillion-parameter model free for a week!
Ant’s Bailing released **Ring-2.6-1T**, a "thinking" giant model designed for complex tasks. It's free to try for a limited one-week period—tech enthusiasts should hurry and give it a go!

🟢 **Baidu ERNIE 5.1**: Only 6% of the cost, but top-tier performance!
Baidu's new-gen large model is here. The key is its "multi-dimensional elastic pre-training," which reportedly cuts training costs to just **6%** of industry peers while maintaining leading performance. It also topped China’s search rankings. Cost efficiency has reached new heights.

🟢 **Open-Source Coding Tool**: Cheaper DeepSeek in the terminal!
New open-source project **Reasonix**, a terminal-specific DeepSeek coding assistant. The highlight? It slashes long-session costs by **80%**! A lifesaver for those coding in the terminal every day.

🟢 **DeepSeek Speeds Up Updates**: V4.1 coming in June!
According to leaks, DeepSeek plans to accelerate model releases, with V4.1 launching next month (June). Looks like the model iteration race is heating up.
