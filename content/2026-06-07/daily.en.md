### SpaceX and Google Sign $920 Million Monthly Cloud Services Agreement

**Event:** SpaceX and Google have reached a cloud services agreement under which Google will pay SpaceX $920 million per month from October 2026 through June 2029 for approximately 110,000 NVIDIA GPUs and related components. Compute capacity will ramp up gradually before September, with corresponding fee adjustments. If SpaceX fails to deliver the agreed GPU volume by September 30, 2026, Google may terminate the agreement or accept the delivered quantity with prorated monthly fees after a one-month grace period. After December 31, 2026, either party may terminate with 90 days' notice. Google retains ownership and intellectual property rights to its content, AI models, and related data.

**Why it matters:** This massive cloud-plus-semiconductor transaction serves as a critical hard signal for assessing GPU supply tightness and AI infrastructure costs.

## Software Engineering

### Microsoft Open Sources pg_durable for Durable Execution Inside PostgreSQL

![Microsoft open sources pg_durable for durable execution inside PostgreSQL](https://github.com/microsoft/pg_durable/raw/main/docs/website/workflow-graph.svg)

**Event:** Microsoft has open-sourced the pg_durable project, a Rust-based PostgreSQL extension enabling developers to implement durable execution directly within the database, integrating business logic execution and data storage in the same layer while avoiding cross-network calls.

**Why it matters:** This tool attempts to "sink compute to the storage layer," significantly reducing distributed transaction latency and complexity by eliminating network roundtrips between application and database layers. It signals databases evolving from pure storage systems into application runtimes with complete business logic execution capabilities, potentially transforming system ingress design under microservices architecture.

### Data Center Developer Switch Negotiating Multi-Billion Dollar Financing at $50B+ Valuation

**Event:** Switch is negotiating a new round of financing in the billions, targeting a post-money valuation of at least $50 billion. Brookfield Asset Management, KKR, and other institutions are in preliminary discussions, though talks remain early-stage and uncertain. If successful, Switch would rank among the world's highest-valued private data center operators, with a valuation approximately five times its 2022 privatization transaction value ($11 billion including debt). It remains unclear whether this valuation includes existing debt. Reports suggest Switch could pursue an IPO as early as next year following the financing, having hired Goldman Sachs and JPMorgan as underwriters. Founded by Rob Roy in 2000, Switch went public in 2017 and was taken private in 2022 by a consortium of DigitalBridge and Australian IFM Investors. SoftBank considered a roughly $50 billion acquisition in late 2025 but abandoned the effort in early 2026 due to concerns over transaction size and operational complexity, having already finalized a $4 billion acquisition of DigitalBridge, a major Switch shareholder.

**Why it matters:** The massive increase in data center valuations reflects strong AI infrastructure demand. The financing scale and potential investors serve as hard signals bearing on the technology platform landscape.
