Today's key takeaway is this: **The AI arms race is shifting from competing on tech and models to a bare-knuckle "capital consumption war."** Whoever can keep burning cash gets to stay at the table.

**[Evidence 1: Titans' Moves (15-35 seconds)]**  
Two solid pieces of evidence back this up. First, look at how the giants are spending today. **SpaceX** and Google signed a staggering contract. Google will pay $920 million per month to secure exclusive access to over a hundred thousand of SpaceX's top-tier GPUs. What does this mean? The most critical computing resources are now locked down by long-term, sky-high agreements. For newcomers wanting to enter? The cost barrier has been raised to the stratosphere.

An even more telling example is **Meta**. To build its AI infrastructure, it's considering raising tens of billions by issuing new stock. This means diluting all existing shareholders' stakes for cash. When the news broke, its stock price dropped immediately. Because the market is starting to question: You're burning through money this fast—when exactly will you start turning a profit?

This signals intense demand but also means the entire ecosystem's cost structure is being reshaped.
