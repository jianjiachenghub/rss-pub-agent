**Podcast Script: Personal Daily — The Bottleneck in the AI Race is Shifting**

**Hosts: A / B**

---

**【Opening】**

**A**: There's a statement from the tech world today that's particularly worth parsing. When NVIDIA CEO Jensen Huang was asked outright whether China should have access to NVIDIA's most advanced chips, he replied with two words: "They shouldn't."

**B**: This stance is actually pivotal. The market had been speculating whether NVIDIA would lobby to loosen restrictions, but now that seems basically off the table. But here's the interesting part: as chip controls tighten, the core bottleneck for AI development is quietly shifting elsewhere.

**A**: Right. Today, the CEO of America's largest power grid announced that the existing grid needs a complete redesign; otherwise, it simply can't handle the power demands of AI data centers. See, we're moving from "scrambling for chips" to "scrambling for electricity"—the very complexion of the AI race is changing.

---

**【Focus 1: Geopolitics and AI Guardrails】**

**A**: Back to Huang's statement—it basically sets the tone for the market. U.S. export controls on high-end AI chips to China are here to stay for the long haul. This means Chinese large-model companies have to reshape their compute cost structures and rely more heavily on domestic alternatives.

**B**: Still, there is some breathing room. There's also news today that both sides are looking to establish "guardrails" for AI competition, to prevent the tech race from spiraling into an uncontrolled crisis. This shows that despite intense confrontation, neither side really wants full decoupling. At least on global issues like AI safety and ethics, the channels for dialogue remain open.

---

**【Focus 2: Energy and Upstream Supply Chains】**

**A**: Speaking of AI infrastructure, I mentioned grid upgrades just now, but the impact is already rippling to the very upstream. Investors are now scrambling to buy companies making specialty glass and data-center cooling systems, because AI servers consume so much power that heat dissipation and infrastructure materials have become the new bottlenecks.

**B**: This investment logic is pretty interesting. Previously, everyone just stared at chip makers like NVIDIA. Now, even traditional manufacturers—anyone remotely connected to data-center cooling and power infrastructure—have become AI concept stocks. Capital is migrating toward the hard infrastructure at the top of the supply chain.

---

**【Focus 3: The Agent Economy’s Closed Loop】**

**A**: While infrastructure is buzzing, the application layer also had a breakthrough today. Amazon AWS added a payment feature to its AI agent service, Bedrock, called AgentCore. This means AI agents can now spend money themselves. Through integrations with Coinbase and Stripe, they can autonomously complete transactions like API calls and service subscriptions.

**B**: This is a pretty fundamental change. Before, AI assistants could only look up information and give recommendations. Now they can spend money and execute actions—essentially upgrading from "advisor" to "purchasing manager." If AI can independently complete commercial closed loops, then the delivery models and pricing logic for software and services have to be completely rewritten.

---

**【Focus 4: Macro Shifts and Capital Costs】**

**A**: Speaking of charging logic, the macro environment is also forcing adjustments. The *Financial Times* ran a piece today arguing that the world is shifting from an era of "savings glut" and cheap money to an era of "capital competition." Governments and corporations worldwide are fiercely competing for funds, and interest rates might stay higher for longer.

**B**: This has huge implications for investment logic. Business models that rely on cheap debt to expand face higher risks, while companies with strong internal cash flows become more attractive. You saw hedge funds post their biggest gains since 2020 today thanks to the tech rebound, but that's actually the market repricing for a "higher-for-longer" rate environment.

---

**【Focus 5: Uncertainty Around Trump Policies】**

**A**: Though whether this risk appetite can continue probably depends on Washington's mood. Signals from Trump were somewhat contradictory today. On one hand, senior aides said they won't "pick winners" in the AI race and will let the market compete freely. On the other hand, he's threatening higher tariffs on European cars and might forcibly take equity stakes in private companies.

**B**: This "Trump capitalism" makes business pretty nervous. Plus, he's exploring potential deals with Iran, so oil prices and stock-market trends hinge on how aggressively he executes his Middle East policy. It feels like markets in the short term largely depend on whether these geopolitical deals actually hold up.

---

**【Closing】**

**A**: Indeed, making investment decisions now requires watching both tech breakthroughs and policy risks simultaneously. From chip controls to energy crises, from agents being able to spend money themselves to rising capital costs, the AI industry is shifting from being purely tech-driven to being driven by both technology and resources.

**B**: Right, and the rules of this game are still evolving. We'll keep watching tomorrow.
