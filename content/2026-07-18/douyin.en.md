Today’s most important takeaway: technology and markets are simultaneously entering a phase of deleveraging and reduced access.

First, technology. OpenAI has confirmed that Codex may accidentally delete user files when sandboxing is disabled and full access is enabled. The message is clear: no matter how powerful coding agents become, they cannot be granted maximum permissions by default. Sandboxing, human review, and data recovery will become non-negotiable requirements for enterprise AI deployment.

Now, global markets. Two oil tankers exploded and caught fire near the Strait of Hormuz, while Iran declared the strait closed. The risk has escalated from verbal threats to physical disruption of shipping. Oil prices, freight rates, and insurance costs could all be repriced.

The third signal comes from South Korea’s stock market: the KOSPI has fallen roughly 25% from its peak, triggering margin calls for 1.2 million accounts. The AI and semiconductor narrative hasn’t disappeared—but the market is starting to ask: how much longer can the leverage hold?

In one sentence: what determines asset prices next won’t be who tells the bigger story, but who has clearer safety boundaries, stronger cash flow, and more reliable execution.
