Title: A Lot Happened Today

Body:

The most important things to watch today are not just about AI, but also energy, central banks, semiconductors, shipping, and market sentiment. Here are the key points:

1. AI power demand is pushing clean energy prices higher  
Clean energy in the U.S. may get more expensive, as AI data centers drive a surge in electricity demand while subsidies are being cut. Going forward, the cost of AI will depend not only on chips, but also on electricity prices.

2. The Fed is being pulled into political games again  
Trump allies want to reshape the Federal Reserve. The point is not the gossip, but that if central bank independence keeps coming under pressure, market expectations around rate cuts, inflation, and dollar assets will become even less stable.

3. AI is starting to enter game editors  
The unity-mcp project on GitHub is getting a lot of attention. It lets AI connect directly to Unity to manage assets, edit scenes, and write scripts. AI coding is moving from “helping you write code” to “helping you operate tools.”

4. South Korea is betting heavily on semiconductors and space  
South Korea has launched an investment plan worth about $204 billion, with Samsung, SK, Hanwha, Hyundai, and others involved. Semiconductors, AI, and space are now core fronts in national-level manufacturing competition.

5. Companies are tightening checks on AI coding tools  
Alibaba will ban Claude Code internally, citing security risks, and require teams to switch to Qoder. Next, when large companies choose AI tools, they will look not only at usability, but also permissions, data security, and supply chain safety.

6. Oil prices and shipping are repricing geopolitical risk  
Some tankers near the Strait of Hormuz have turned around, while rising crude supply has pushed oil prices lower. Energy markets are now watching both geopolitical routes and oversupply.

7. Local AI tools are getting more attention  
Local AI meeting assistants like Meetily are starting to emerge, supporting on-device transcription and summaries. For legal, financial, and R&D teams, “data stays on the machine” will become increasingly important.

One-sentence summary:  
Today’s main thread is that AI is spilling over into electricity, software tools, security reviews, and industrial investment; meanwhile, energy, central banks, and markets are also repricing risk. Don’t just stare at one hot topic. The real big shifts are happening at the intersections.
