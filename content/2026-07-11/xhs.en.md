# Today’s Must-Read: AI Is Surging—and So Are the Risks

If today could be summed up in one sentence: AI investment continues to accelerate, but constraints around chips, copyright, talent, financing, and energy have now fully surfaced.

🧠 AI and Chips: The Memory Shortage Could Last Beyond 2030

SK Hynix believes the expansion of AI and data centers could extend the global memory shortage beyond 2030, with customers already beginning to sign long-term contracts.

This suggests the memory industry may no longer follow its traditional boom-and-bust cycle every few years. High-end products such as HBM could retain strong pricing power over the long term. However, companies plan to expand capacity aggressively through 2034, potentially causing the market to swing rapidly from shortage to surplus.

Meanwhile, the United States is preparing to ease restrictions on advanced chip exports to the UAE. If implemented, the policy could make the Gulf a new growth market for high-end computing—but actual shipment volumes will still depend on end-use reviews.

🤖 OpenAI: Upgrading Models, Agents, and the Work Hub Together

OpenAI has launched the GPT-5.6 series, with Sol, Terra, and Luna targeting flagship, balanced, and low-cost needs. The flagship version can also coordinate four AI agents by default to handle complex tasks in parallel.

More notably, ChatGPT, productivity features, and Codex are being integrated into a single desktop hub. AI competition is no longer just about who has the strongest model, but who can become the gateway to users’ daily search, office, and development workflows.

⚖️ Apple and OpenAI: From Partners to Courtroom Rivals

Apple has sued OpenAI, accusing former employees of taking confidential information about unreleased products, components, and supply chains. It is demanding that OpenAI stop using and destroy the materials—and even redesign related products.

This is more than an ordinary talent dispute. Reports say more than 400 former Apple employees have joined OpenAI. Whether the court issues an injunction could directly affect the design, supply chain, and launch timeline of OpenAI’s hardware.

🎨 Meta Pauses AI Image Generation: Public Content ≠ Free to Generate With

Meta has paused a feature that used content from public Instagram accounts to generate images. Its default opt-in and opt-out mechanisms had drawn criticism from talent agencies and others.

The incident is a warning for the entire industry: publicly available content does not mean platforms automatically have permission to use it for generation. AI products will need clearer licensing mechanisms and will face higher costs for content review and compliance.

💰 Markets: Capital Keeps Chasing AI, but Patience Is Wearing Thin

SK Hynix completed a record $26.5 billion U.S. share offering, rising 13% on its first day of trading—a sign that investors are still willing to bet on long-term demand for AI memory.

On the other hand, investors have begun selling long-dated AI-related bonds. Tech giants’ heavy borrowing to build computing centers is increasing long-term credit risk. The upcoming earnings season will also be a crucial test: even record profits may not prevent highly valued stocks from falling if they miss the market’s lofty expectations.

🛢️ Geopolitics and Energy: Iran Tensions Continue to Raise Risks

Iran has refused to negotiate with the United States under the current conditions, while the U.S. has escalated threats and sanctions, further weakening prospects for a ceasefire.

As shipping risks in the Persian Gulf rise, China has asked some major refiners to maintain high fuel output. This can safeguard supplies in the short term, but if crude oil costs continue climbing, refiners’ margins could come under pressure.

📉 Macroeconomic Policy: Interest Rates and Pricing Mechanisms Are Both Shifting

Brazilian inflation cooled more than expected, creating room for another interest-rate cut.

In the United States, markets are watching not only interest rates but also whether the Federal Reserve will adjust its communication and policy implementation framework. The Trump administration continues to pressure companies to lower prices, potentially deepening government involvement in corporate pricing and profit allocation.

💻 Open Source: PostgreSQL Rewrite in Rust Crosses a Major Threshold

The popular pgrust project is reimplementing PostgreSQL in Rust and says it has passed the original Postgres regression test suite in full.

This shifts the question from “Can a rewrite be done?” to “Can it be used in production?” The real deciding factors will be extension compatibility, performance, the operations ecosystem, and long-term upgrade costs.

Three key takeaways from today:

1. AI expansion is turning memory, computing power, and financing into long-term competitions.
2. Apple’s lawsuit against OpenAI and Meta’s pause on AI image generation show that talent and content licensing are becoming hard constraints for AI products.
3. Iran tensions, shifting interest rates, and earnings pressure remind markets that beyond the technology narrative, energy and macroeconomic risks matter just as much.
