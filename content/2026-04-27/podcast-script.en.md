**[Opening: The Main Story]**

**A:** We have a pretty interesting contrast today. On one hand, you have OpenAI, the big player in the large model space, finally loosening its ties with Microsoft to explore more of its own commercial paths. On the other, you have the homegrown DeepSeek, which, at its new version launch, actually came out and said, "we're still about 3 to 6 months behind GPT-5.4."

**B:** It's definitely an interesting dynamic when you put those two stories together. Everyone used to think that foundational models had to be deeply tied to a cloud platform, just like OpenAI and Microsoft. Now OpenAI is seeking independence beyond Azure, while DeepSeek is deliberately taking a more humble stance on its technology. It feels like the entire industry is shifting from storytelling to crunching the numbers.

---

**[Item 1: Developer Tools]**

**A:** Speaking of crunching the numbers, GitHub Copilot is also changing its pricing model. Starting in June 2026, it's switching from the old fixed monthly fee to a pay-per-token usage model.

**B:** This has a real impact on developers. Before, it was the same price no matter how much or how little you used it. Now, heavy users might have to keep a close eye on their usage limits. It's basically passing the inference costs of the underlying large model directly to the end-user. Development costs go from fixed to variable, and teams will have to re-evaluate how they budget for AI-assisted programming.

---

**[Item 2: Diverging AI Regulation]**

**A:** Now, looking at the policy side, the UK has made it clear it doesn't want to align with the EU's AI Act. It's leaning toward the more relaxed US approach to regulation.

**B:** This basically confirms a three-way split in global AI regulation: strict in the EU, relaxed in the US, and the UK trying to find a middle path to attract business. For multinational companies, compliance costs are definitely going up. But the UK is likely trying to create a relatively permissive sandbox for its local AI companies, to compete with the EU.

---

**[Item 3: Geopolitics]**

**A:** There's also an escalation on the geopolitical front. The US has started sanctioning Chinese oil refineries, trying to apply pressure by disrupting the supply chain.

**B:** This marks a shift in signaling. Sanctions used to be focused mainly on the tech sector, but now they're extending to fundamental industrial nodes like energy. This won't just change the cost structure of China's energy sector; it also redefines where the "red lines" are drawn in international trade.

---

**[Item 4: Financial Markets]**

**A:** The financial markets are also sending signals today. Star fund manager Bobby Jain's new fund, Jain Global, is already returning money to outside investors just a few months after launching, and converting into a vehicle that only manages money for Millennium.

**B:** This shows that even for top-tier fund managers, raising new money from the market is extremely difficult right now. The enthusiasm for multi-strategy platforms is cooling down, and capital is tending to flow back to more established institutions. It's kind of a report card on the risk appetite in the hedge fund industry, and it also affects capital flows across the entire sector.

---

**[Item 5: Open-Source Tech]**

**A:** Lastly, a neat little item from the tech world. Solon AI released a new version of its agent framework for Java developers, which focuses on being "restrained and efficient." It's compatible with everything from Java 8 to 26, and it also integrates the MCP protocol.

**B:** The AI development toolchain is getting more and more specialized. This kind of full-stack framework, aimed at veteran Java programmers, is actually lowering the barrier to entry for implementing AI applications. It abstracts away model differences on the high end and manages things like streaming and vector databases on the low end. Pretty pragmatic.

---

**[Closing]**

**A:** Looking at all these stories together today—whether it's OpenAI seeking independence, DeepSeek admitting its gap, or Copilot changing its billing—they all point to the same trend: the AI industry is moving away from being tied to big platforms and telling grand narratives, and shifting toward more detailed commercial calculations and technical pragmatism.

**B:** Right. Even regulation and geopolitics are adjusting their boundaries accordingly. This "cool-down period" might be tougher to get through than the initial frenzy, but it's also a true test of who has what it takes. See you tomorrow.
