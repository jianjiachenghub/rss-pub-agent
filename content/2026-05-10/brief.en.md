Today's View: Geopolitical risks are rapidly reshaping the global macro narrative, with inflation and monetary policy expectations facing sharp adjustments.

Three Key Signals:
1. **Macro Shift**: PIMCO warns that energy and supply chain inflation triggered by Iran conflict could force the Fed to raise rates instead of cutting them, reversing market consensus.
2. **Escalation**: U.S. military deployed over 20 warships for a maritime blockade against Iran, pushing the conflict into a combat phase. Security of navigation through the Strait of Hormuz has become a direct risk factor for oil prices and global supply chains.
3. **Supply Contraction**: Saudi Aramco reports that global oil supply has dropped sharply by about 1 billion barrels over the past two months. Supply-side factors have become a core driver pushing up oil prices, intensifying inflationary pressures.

What to Watch Next: Diplomatic mediation in the Iran situation (e.g., Iran's response to U.S. proposals) and the actual traffic conditions in the Strait of Hormuz—these will serve as key levers for short-term market sentiment.
