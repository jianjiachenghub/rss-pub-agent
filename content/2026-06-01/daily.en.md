# Personal Daily | June 1, 2026

## Today's Take

> The compute arms race has shifted from cloud data centers to personal endpoints. Trillion-dollar deployments by NVIDIA and OpenAI signal that the hardware war has entered a war of attrition. Yet capital markets are showing structural divergence: hedge funds are making their most aggressive bets on U.S. equities in six months, while compute infrastructure must rely on junk-bond financing. Bain's warning on below-expectation AI returns adds to the alarm. The core question is whether the AI productivity narrative can support higher rate tolerance and sunk capital costs—or if this marks the final stage of the party.

## Watch Signals

Three variables require close monitoring going forward: First, subscription appetite and spread movements for high-risk debt such as CoreWeave's issues, to gauge risk appetite thresholds; second, actual AI contributions to margins in corporate earnings, to validate whether the ROI narrative holds; third, whether the Fed incorporates AI supply-side effects into inflation framework adjustments. The directional resonance of these three signals will determine whether the current capital expenditure frenzy marks the start of a new cycle or the peak of a bubble.
