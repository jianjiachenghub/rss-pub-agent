**Podcast Script: Tech's New Order Under a Three-Way Game**

---

**[Opening]**

**A:** I think today's episode has one really clear thread running through it—technology, capital, and geopolitics are engaged in a remarkably direct three-way game.

**B:** Right. These three used to be discussed in separate lanes. But look at today—from cross-border AI model access restrictions, to SpaceX's trillion-dollar IPO, to the deleveraging in chip stocks—they've all suddenly been crammed into the same time window.

---

**[Segment 1: Anthropic's Geopolitical Lockout]**

**A:** Let's start with the Anthropic situation. After the U.S. government barred foreigners from accessing cutting-edge AI models, Anthropic straight-up suspended services for non-U.S. users.

**B:** That's fascinating. We used to say AI has national borders, but it was mostly a metaphor. Now it's a hard rule. Claude had been the go-to workaround for a lot of domestic developers, and now that door is closing too.

**A:** Exactly. This shows AI model accessibility has shifted from a technical issue to a direct geopolitical tool. From now on, we'll probably have to look at AI development in terms of a U.S. version, a China version, and an everyone-else version.

---

**[Segment 2: SpaceX IPO and Capital Concentration]**

**B:** Speaking of great-power rivalry, capital seems to recognize only one winner. SpaceX's market cap soared to $2.1 trillion on its first trading day, making Musk the world's first trillionaire.

**A:** And what's coming with this is the "siphoning effect"—other space stocks are getting hammered as capital frees up positions to buy into SpaceX.

**B:** Classic Matthew Effect. When an industry leader emerges, the secondary market doesn't want "diversity," it wants "certainty." But this also shows the exit channels for hard-tech investing are opening up—look at Prometheus, the one Bezos backed. It just raised $12 billion in its Series B at a valuation of over $40 billion. They're using AI to accelerate hardware R&D.

---

**[Segment 3: A Rewrite of AI Valuation Logic]**

**A:** That signal is crucial. AI funding used to flow mainly to the "soft" side—content generation. Now capital is making big bets on the "hard" stuff: robotics, drug design, chip manufacturing.

**B:** And isn't it striking? Word came out today that OpenAI is slashing prices to grab market share. Meanwhile, China's Moonshot AI—monthly active users are actually declining—but its valuation is reportedly jumping from $4.3 billion to $30 billion.

**A:** This completely breaks the valuation logic of the Internet era. It used to be about user numbers; now it's about technical moats and commercialization ability. We're seeing a strategic split between Chinese and American AI companies: one side is burning cash for scale, the other is commanding a premium for technical barriers.

---

**[Segment 4: Wall Street Tightens Chip Leverage]**

**B:** Speaking of the physical industries, Wall Street is tightening leverage on chip stocks. Citi and Goldman are both restricting hedge funds from going long on SK Hynix and TSMC through swap contracts.

**A:** That's a telling detail. Chip stocks used to be a magnet for leveraged money. Now that investment banks are proactively raising financing costs and capping size, it means they believe short-term correction risk is building.

**B:** Exactly. When capital is doing two things at once—chasing the ultimate dream like SpaceX while tightening leverage on mature supply chains—that's a clear sign of narrowing risk appetite. The money is still in tech, but it's pickier.

---

**[Segment 5: HarmonyOS Goes Agent-Native]**

**A:** Speaking of technical moats, Huawei's newly released HarmonyOS 7 is worth watching for developers. They've built the Agent architecture directly into the OS layer, not just as a top-level application.

**B:** This could change the development paradigm. Instead of building apps, developers may have to pivot to "intent-based service development." And this is the true entry point for on-device AI—when cloud-based AI is being choked by geopolitics, the autonomy of on-device AI becomes more important than ever.

---

**[Segment 6: Changxin Memory Technologies IPO]**

**A:** Finally, a related note. The CSRC has approved Changxin Memory Technologies' IPO on the STAR Market. The domestic memory-chip leader is finally coming.

**B:** The timing is remarkably subtle. On one side, Wall Street is tightening chip leverage; on the other, domestic regulators are opening financing channels for semiconductor firms. After Changxin lists, A-share investors can finally invest directly in domestic DRAM. Its performance will become a barometer for the entire domestic semiconductor sector.

---

**[Closing]**

**B:** So you see, however we circle around it, the core of today's episode is this: when technology becomes a geopolitical bargaining chip, capital starts recalculating risk premiums, and companies can only search for new architectures and business models in the cracks.

**A:** Exactly. Whether it's Anthropic suspending services, Huawei going Agent-native, or SpaceX hitting a trillion-dollar valuation, they're all telling us: the era of pure technology is over. Now it's a three-body problem of tech, geopolitics, and capital.
