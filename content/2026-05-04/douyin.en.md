(Hook) The ultimate endgame of AI might not be technology, but capital.

Today’s most critical observation is: the business models of major AI companies are undergoing a fundamental shift. They are no longer content with selling APIs. Instead, they are teaming up directly with financial capital—armed with money and clients—to carve up the enterprise market together.

This shift rests on two core pillars:
First, **top-tier AI companies are deeply aligning with Wall Street.** Anthropic has formed a service joint venture with Blackstone and Goldman Sachs, and OpenAI plans to launch a $10 billion joint venture with private equity. This means which AI a mid-sized company uses in the future may not be decided by the CTO, but by the PE fund behind it.
Second, **AI has become a national-level strategic reserve.** European finance ministers are pressuring Anthropic for access to its latest Mythos model for national security-level attack and defense exercises. This shows the capability boundaries of the strongest AIs are being redefined; they are no longer just tools, but are being treated as quasi-strategic weapons.

Once these two trends converge, they will completely change the rules of the game in the AI industry: tech companies build the guns, financial capital locks in the customers, and national powers draw the red lines.
