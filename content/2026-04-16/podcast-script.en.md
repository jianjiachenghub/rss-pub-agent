**(01:25 - 02:35) AI Battlefield: Shifting Offense and Defense, Tightening Regulation**

**Host B:** Enough about money—let's look at the tech frontier. There's a serious topic in AI right now: cybersecurity. The Economist predicts that in the short term, AI technology actually favors the attackers.

**Host A:** That's true. AI-powered automated attacks have lowered both the barrier and the cost of doing harm. Companies are being forced to shift their security budgets from passive defense to active countermeasures. This isn't just a technical issue—it's fundamentally changing the cost structure of defense.

**Host B:** Under that kind of pressure, regulators can't just sit still either. Policymakers in the US are now waking up to AI's dangerous potential—they've realized that the old "hands-off" attitude just doesn't work anymore.

**Host A:** That's an important signal. Going forward, US legislation on AI model safety testing and liability assignment is going to accelerate. For AI companies, compliance costs are definitely going up, and product launch cycles will get longer.

**Host B:** Speaking of which, we have to mention Anthropic. They rolled out large-scale identity verification—that's KYC—in Claude. A lot of users complained it felt like a betrayal, but objectively, it's opened a window for domestic large language models to grab market share.

**Host B:** Right. Whether you're a giant or a startup, you're now fighting on two fronts: channels and compliance.
