**A:** Hello everyone, welcome to today's Personal Daily. I'm A.

**B:** I'm B. Today's main storyline is really interesting. It feels like the "honeymoon phase" between tech giants is quietly coming to an end, and everyone is busy securing their own backup plans.

**A:** Exactly. The most typical signal comes from Microsoft. Although they have a close relationship with OpenAI, according to the latest news, Microsoft plans to build a fully in-house, cutting-edge large AI model by 2027—that's within the next two or three years.

**B:** The intention is just too obvious. Microsoft AI CEO Suleyman has even stated that they want to build "alternatives" for core capabilities like text and image processing, so they aren't held hostage by others regarding key capabilities. This basically tells everyone: whether it's compute power or models, vertical integration is the future moat.

**A:** This shift is reflected even more directly in the capital markets. Recently, there was an interesting phenomenon in the secondary market: about 600 million dollars worth of OpenAI shares were up for sale but found no takers, while shares of their competitor, Anthropic, were being snatched up like crazy, with institutions lining up with billions in cash to get in.

**B:** Even Musk jumped out to say he was "not surprised at all." It looks like investors are reassessing risk and reward. After all, OpenAI's valuation is already sky-high, while Anthropic is catching up fast. This valuation gap is driving capital's choice.

**A:** Moving back to technology itself, besides the battle of giants, developers are making moves too. AMD released an open-source tool called Lemonade that allows local PCs to run large models using GPUs and NPUs.

**B:** That is very practical. AMD is trying to bypass Nvidia's CUDA blockade and lower the barrier for local deployment. In the future, running a local model on your own computer might be as simple as installing regular software.

**A:** The model layer is also seeing intense competition. Alibaba released Qwen3.6-Plus. The focus this time isn't just simple text generation, but aiming at "real-world agents," with significantly stronger reasoning and task execution capabilities.

**B:** Right, and its coding performance reportedly beats models two or three times its size. This shows that open-source models are rapidly closing the gap with top closed-source models on complex tasks like Agents. This is good news for research implementation.

**A:** In terms of application implementation, the financial sector is moving fast. Rocket Close partnered with AWS to automate document processing for mortgages. Documents that used to require manual review are now handled directly by machines.

**B:** Processing unstructured documents used to be a huge cost center for banks, but now AI is compressing that cost. There's something even more cutting-edge: payment giants like Coinbase and Stripe are joining forces to build "wallets" for AI agents.

**A:** Exactly. Future transactions might not need human intervention. AI agents will be able to complete payments and trades on their own. This is actually defining the financial gateway of the future internet—whoever sets the standard holds the initiative.

**B:** Speaking of gateways and data, OpenAI recently acquired a tech podcast, TBPN. It sounds like a crossover, but I think the core reason is high-quality data. Public text data is running dry, and exclusive content like podcasts forms a new data barrier.

**A:** True, data is fuel. The application data domestically is also astonishing. The daily Token usage of the Doubao large model has exceeded 120 trillion, doubling in just three months. This growth rate shows that enterprise-side adoption is really picking up.

**B:** Moving on from tech, let's take a quick look at other sectors. Qatar Airways announced the resumption of flights from Doha to domestic cities like Hangzhou and Chongqing, but our domestic airlines haven't followed suit yet. This mismatch in capacity supply is quite noteworthy.

**A:** On the geopolitical front, after Trump's remarks regarding Iran, oil prices surged, and US stocks ended their two-day winning streak. Market sentiment is still tense; even the slightest ripple has an impact.

**B:** Finally, I want to leave the audience with a deeper thought: with AI-generated content increasing, if future models are trained on AI-produced content, will it lead to "model collapse," actually causing human technology to stagnate?

**A:** There's really no conclusion on this yet. Data quality determines the model's ceiling, which conversely explains why everyone values exclusive, high-quality human data sources so much right now.
