Title: Today Is About More Than AI

Body:

There was a lot of information today, but the main thread is actually clear: AI continues to reshape technology, energy, finance, and geopolitics, while central bank independence, exchange rates, and antitrust are also changing market expectations.

1. Claude access in China keeps tightening  
Anthropic is reportedly restricting Chinese users’ access to Claude, and Alibaba has also told employees internally to stop using Claude, Sonnet, Opus, and Claude Code, recommending its in-house Qoder as an alternative.  
The key point is not “which model is stronger,” but that companies choosing AI tools in the future need to factor in access stability, compliance, security review, and migration costs together.

2. AI is increasing pressure on power and infrastructure  
This summer, the U.S. is facing high temperatures, drought, and surging data center electricity demand at the same time, pushing the power grid to its limits. CPP has also invested $1.75 billion in EQT’s AI infrastructure projects.  
AI competition is no longer just about models. It is also a race in electricity, storage, data centers, and supply chains.

3. AI compute stocks were rattled by Meta  
Meta plans to sell excess AI compute capacity to external customers. After the news, AI infrastructure stocks such as Nvidia, TSMC, and AMD fell, while Meta rose.  
This shows the market is starting to ask a question: are leading companies really short on compute, or has some compute already become temporarily surplus?

4. The Fed is becoming a political variable  
Trump and his allies continue to push for reshaping the Federal Reserve. Although the court blocked the dismissal of Lisa Cook, the fight is not over.  
Going forward, markets will be watching not only interest rate data, but also Fed independence, board appointments, and the succession of the chair.

5. AI capital continues to flow toward Asia and China  
Hong Kong is seen as becoming a key hub for Asia’s $2 trillion AI trade; Chinese quant funds are attracting capital thanks to AI-driven strategies; and Kunlunxin is nearing a Hong Kong listing, targeting a valuation of about $50 billion.  
AI is reshaping capital flows. Whoever controls data, compute, chips, and trading capabilities will have an easier time attracting capital.

6. Enterprise AI is entering the “implementation battle”  
Microsoft has formed a new department of around 6,000 people to accelerate AI adoption among enterprise customers and within its own business. Alibaba has also integrated three major enterprise Agent products, concentrating resources on to-B productivity tools.  
The next stage of competition among AI companies may not be won at launch events, but on enterprise desktops, in development workflows, and inside business systems.

7. Other things worth watching  
Google lost its appeal against the EU’s €4.1 billion Android antitrust fine, showing EU regulation remains tough.  
The yen fell to a historic low, with markets watching Japan’s policy response.  
Podman 6.0 was released, with its network stack shifting to Netavark and nftables.  
Vite+ Beta was released, integrating frontend development, testing, building, and checking into one command.

One key line for today:  
AI is still expanding, but the market has moved from “believing in growth” to “questioning costs, regulation, supply, and real-world implementation.”
