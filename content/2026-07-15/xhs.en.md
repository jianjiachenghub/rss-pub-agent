# Today’s Must-Reads Go Beyond AI

July 15 brought a flood of developments: AI safety, chip capacity expansion, Apple’s AI rollout in China, the energy crisis, U.S. market liquidity, and the battle over rare earths all intensified at once. Here are the six key themes👇

**1️⃣ AI enters the “attack itself, upgrade itself” era**

OpenAI has launched GPT-Red, which continuously generates attacks through self-play and searches for defenses. Model safety is moving from human testing toward a fully automated offensive-defensive loop.

Meanwhile, some users have noticed weaker performance from GPT-5.6 Sol on complex tasks, with the community attributing it to a hidden reduction in its “reasoning budget.” Going forward, judging a model’s capabilities may require looking beyond the version number to how much compute the platform actually allocates.

**2️⃣ Another warning over the data boundaries of coding agents**

Security researchers reported that Grok Build CLI silently uploaded entire Git repositories, prompting xAI to disable the feature overnight.

This is a reminder to every developer: before giving an AI coding tool access to a project, always check its file permissions, network requests, and telemetry scope. The truly sensitive data is not the chat history—it is the entire codebase.

**3️⃣ Apple Intelligence in China finally clears a key hurdle**

Apple Intelligence and other on-device generative AI services have completed regulatory registration. The Chinese version will integrate Alibaba’s Qwen and cover all four of Apple’s major operating systems.

The key questions are no longer about whether it can launch, but:

- When it will officially become available
- Whether it will gain system-level access
- How widely Qwen will actually be used across Apple’s ecosystem

**4️⃣ The AI boom is redistributing industry profits**

ASML raised its outlook, showing that advanced-node capacity expansion continues to benefit equipment suppliers. ChangXin Memory Technologies reportedly plans to raise around $10 billion, signaling that memory-chip expansion is also attracting large-scale capital.

Meanwhile, IBM warned that customer spending is shifting from traditional software toward AI hardware and storage, sending its shares down more than 25% in a single day. AI is not lifting every technology company—it is reshaping how budgets and valuations are divided.

DeepSeek is also reportedly planning an IPO in Shanghai as early as the second quarter of next year. Competition among leading model companies is expanding from a technology race into a capital race.

**5️⃣ The Strait of Hormuz becomes the biggest variable for global markets**

After the strait was closed again, traders warned that spot supplies were approaching their breaking point. With military tensions targeting a chokepoint for global energy shipments, oil prices, inflation, and interest-rate expectations could all be disrupted at once.

The Bundesbank president still argues that the European Central Bank should hold off on raising rates, while Federal Reserve Chair Warsh has reiterated that high inflation will not be tolerated. Central banks now face a difficult dilemma: an energy shock has arrived, but tightening too early could also damage the economy.

**6️⃣ Liquidity pressure is building beneath the U.S. stock market boom**

Morgan Stanley’s profit rose 58% year over year, while BlackRock attracted $192 billion in quarterly net inflows and surpassed $15 trillion in assets under management for the first time—evidence that trading and capital allocation remain active.

But heavy fundraising by major companies such as SpaceX, Alphabet, and SK Hynix could also absorb substantial market liquidity. The next force shaping U.S. stocks will not just be earnings and interest rates, but an easily overlooked variable: new equity supply.

Meanwhile, the EU has begun assembling a crisis-response team for rare-earth supply risks, while legislation imposing tariffs on buyers of Russian energy is also advancing. Critical minerals, energy, and trade tools are becoming increasingly intertwined with geopolitical competition.

**In one sentence:**

The real theme to watch today is AI’s shift from a “technology story” into the real world—it is changing security rules, corporate budgets, chip investment, and capital markets, while energy corridors, rare-earth supplies, and equity financing are emerging as new sources of global risk.
