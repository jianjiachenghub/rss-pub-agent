This is crucial, because in the future, what investors and buyers look at may no longer be “do you have an AI slogan,” but whether models have entered customer service, R&D, workflows, and decision-making systems.

Host A: This also explains the second story: AI’s impact on employment has already reached Ireland’s tech sector. Ireland used to attract U.S. multinational tech companies, bringing in a large number of tech jobs. But now some operations, support, and junior technical roles are starting to be cut as AI improves efficiency.

Host B: What is changing here is the site-selection logic of multinationals. In the past, they looked at taxes, labor costs, and talent supply. Now they also have to look at automation efficiency. If AI can replace some offshore roles, then the so-called “cost advantage” will be recalculated.

Host A: The financial industry is also under pressure at the same time. UK regulators warned that AI adoption in financial services is accelerating, and the regulatory side is trying to catch up with the pace of industry deployment.

Host B: This is not purely a technical issue. If banks, asset managers, and insurers use AI in risk control, trading, customer service, and compliance judgments, regulators must understand how models make decisions and how risks spread. If the industry moves fast and regulators lag one step behind, systemic risks become harder to identify.

Host A: On the model side, Tencent Hunyuan Hy3 has officially been open-sourced. Host B: Nvidia has also open-sourced Nemotron-Labs-TwoTower, a discrete diffusion language model with 60B total parameters, split into two 30B models, and it reuses Nemotron’s pretrained weights to reduce training costs. You can see that model competition is not just about bigger parameter counts, but also training efficiency, inference structure, and usability.

Host A: But progress on AI Agents has not been as smooth as imagined. Reuters cited an internal Meta meeting recording saying that Zuckerberg admitted the development trajectory of AI Agents over the past four months had not accelerated as expected.

Host B: That is very realistic. For Agents to be truly usable, they cannot just chat. They need to reliably call tools, remember context, handle exceptions, and execute dependably inside enterprise workflows. Industry enthusiasm is very high right now, but the barriers to implementation are also becoming clearer.

Host A: On the developer tools side, Vercel launched the skills command-line tool, allowing AI capability packs to be installed with one line: `npx skills add`. It officially supports more than 68 agents, and the repository has gained 24,000 stars in five months.

Host B: Behind this is a change in how Agent capabilities are distributed. In the past, people copied prompts and borrowed configurations. Now it is more like installing plugins, installing packages, and checking rankings. In the future, the stickiness of developer tools may come from an installable, reusable skills marketplace.

Host A: But the cost issue has also arrived. SemiAnalysis says internal large-model token spending has already reached 30% of total employee salaries, with nearly 5 billion tokens consumed per person per month. Host B: This shows that AI bills are no longer ordinary software subscription fees, but more like cloud compute. A decline in token unit prices does not mean total spending will necessarily fall. What enterprises need to do next is quota management, auditing, ROI accounting, and distinguishing which AI usage actually improves output.

Host A: On markets and macro, two stories today both point to rising expectations for global interest rates. Host B: The core variable is not short-term ups and downs in oil prices, but the energy risk premium, inflation expectations, and central bank reaction functions. If policy uncertainty overlaps with energy price volatility, the timing and magnitude of rate cuts become harder to confirm in advance, and global assets will also demand a higher risk premium.

Host A: There is also a signal in the crypto market. Michael Saylor’s Strategy sold $216 million worth of Bitcoin last week, its first major move after disclosing its financing restructuring plan.

Host B: What this changes is the liquidity assumption around Bitcoin treasury companies. In the past, “only buy, never sell” was a valuation narrative. But if both the stock price and Bitcoin price remain under pressure for a long time, the company has to face refinancing, debt management, and cash flow constraints.

Host B: This reminds us that a rising index does not mean the strategy environment is stable. The faster style rotation becomes, the more easily models that rely on historical momentum and crowded trades can suffer drawdowns. Today, from AI to macro to markets, the main thread is actually the same: the narrative is still there, but what really matters is whether it can be verified, and whether the numbers add up.
