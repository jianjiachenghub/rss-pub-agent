**Podcast Script: Personal Daily Briefing**

**Hosts:** A (The Rational Analyst), B (The Insightful Observer)
**Total Duration:** Approx. 4 mins 30 secs

---

**[Opening]**

**A:** Let's start by zeroing in on the main story of the day. Early this morning, Apple pushed Apple Intelligence to iPhone users in China—that's the "Apple AI" everyone has been waiting for.

**B:** Right, it has finally landed. I saw quite a few users testing it after updating, and it looks like under the hood, it’s calling Baidu's Ernie Bot model. But what’s interesting is that the login interface for the ChatGPT extension is still preserved in the settings.

**A:** That is actually a very important signal. This hybrid architecture of "local compliant model + external extension" basically establishes the regulatory boundary for cross-border AI products landing in China. For big models looking to enter the domestic market in the future, this might just be the standard answer.

**B:** Exactly. For Baidu, this means securing default distribution rights on hundreds of millions of mobile devices. That is incredibly high value.

---

**[Segment One: Internet Ecosystem]**

**A:** Now that we’ve talked about entry points, let’s look at a somewhat surprising set of data. HUMAN Security released a report saying that the growth rate of automated traffic on the internet is now eight times that of human activity.

**B:** That extreme?

**A:** Yes. And in terms of total volume, bot traffic has officially surpassed real human users. This means the fundamental nature of the internet has changed; "real users" are no longer the default assumption for traffic.

**B:** This has a massive impact on the business environment. Ad billing models, anti-fraud systems, and data cleaning logic will likely all have to be torn up and started over. The operational costs for platforms just to verify "you are human" are going to skyrocket.

---

**[Segment Two: Developers & Tools]**

**A:** There’s also movement on the tools front. Yesterday, Alibaba’s Qwen released the omni-modal large model Qwen3.5-Omni. This series is very comprehensive, supporting ultra-long context and capable of handling 10 hours of audio input.

**B:** So they’ve fully integrated listening, speaking, and seeing. This saves developers a lot of trouble; they don't need to go around stitching together various single-purpose models. Also, a developer named Ahmed Nagdy created a site called Learn Claude Code, which is pretty interesting.

**A:** How so?

**B:** It provides 11 interactive modules where you can practice right in the browser’s terminal simulator without setting up a local environment. This "zero-barrier to entry" approach is becoming the standard for AI programming tool promotion—it's all about accelerating the migration from traditional IDEs to AI Agent programming.

---

**[Segment Three: Workplace & Talent]**

**A:** Speaking of Agent programming, this actually leads to a rather heavy topic. I saw an article in today’s report discussing engineer growth, arguing that AI is "eating the middle layer."

**B:** I noticed that too. The argument was sharp: those basic tasks engineers typically do in the first ten years of their careers, AI can now handle very well. But those repetitive tasks were precisely the "practice mechanism" for cultivating judgment and intuition.

**A:** Exactly. It’s like the lower rungs of the ladder are broken. If entry-level positions disappear, companies might face an awkward situation later: they won’t be able to cultivate senior architects with systemic judgment. I even saw a tech lead say that because AI is performing so well, he cancelled campus recruitment.

**B:** This really isn't just a cost issue; it’s a risk of a broken talent development pipeline.

---

**[Segment Four: Business & Capital]**

**A:** Let’s switch to a lighter topic and look at the capital markets. Over in Hong Kong stocks, the new IPO for Fourier saw oversubscription exceed 3,000 times.

**B:** Capital is still surging into hard tech; it became this year's oversubscription king in the chip sector. This echoes Marc Andreessen’s recent point on a podcast. He feels that of the massive value generated by AI, 99% might be captured by an extremely small number of entities.

**A:** This is actually a reminder to investors: while the application layer is lively, the core value is likely still monopolized by infrastructure and the top model layers.

**B:** Right. But there are also chaotic aspects. That Google paper which crashed global storage stocks has been accused of academic misconduct by Chinese scholars. They say it avoided mentioning similarities with other algorithms. This has caused quite a stir in academic circles, and Google hasn't fully corrected it yet.

---

**[Segment Five: Frontier Thinking]**

**A:** In the time we have left, let’s turn to mathematician Terence Tao. He posted a long article specifically exploring mathematical methods in the AI era.

**B:** He is focused on the trust mechanism within AI-assisted proofs. The fact that top mathematicians are getting involved shows that AI is truly changing the research verification cycle in basic disciplines. Verifying AI-generated content is going to become standard practice in academia.

**A:** Indeed. From the landing of mobile entry points, to the reversal of the internet's main entity, to the breaking of the career ladder and the reshaping of academic standards—AI is penetrating deeper than we imagined.

**B:** Alright, that’s it for today’s Personal Daily Briefing. See you tomorrow.

**A:** See you tomorrow.
