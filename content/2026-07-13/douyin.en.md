Before you call it an AI bubble, money is still pouring into computing power.

Today’s key takeaway: AI capital spending remains strong, but market risks are spreading beyond the tech race into energy and policy.

The clearest evidence? TSMC’s quarterly sales surged 36% year over year. Meanwhile, Intel plans to invest €5 billion in its Irish plant. Capital is still betting on advanced manufacturing, packaging, and high-bandwidth memory—showing no clear signs of cooling demand for AI chips.

But when companies buy models, they’re paying much closer attention to ROI. More and more are adopting lower-cost Chinese models. The competition is shifting from “Who’s the most powerful?” to “Who’s cheaper and easier to use?” The U.S. may also expand its restrictions beyond chips and model weights—to API outputs and training behavior.

The biggest external variable is the Strait of Hormuz. As conflict escalates in the Middle East, oil has climbed above $80 a barrel. If prices stay high, markets may have to reprice both inflation and expectations for rate cuts.

So watch three things next: TSMC’s orders, the model price war, and whether the Strait of Hormuz remains open to shipping.
