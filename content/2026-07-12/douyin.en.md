Heads up—the AI market is shifting gears. The next race may no longer be about who rises fastest, but who can actually make money.

Today’s key takeaway: the market is moving from “chasing the AI narrative” to testing AI’s unit economics and ability to deliver real profits.

First, much of the emerging-market rally has been driven by three tech giants worth roughly $4.4 trillion combined. Some funds are already looking beyond the AI leaders. That means capital could rotate out of a handful of heavyweight stocks and into sectors that have been overshadowed.

Second, MetaX’s share price briefly topped 1,000 yuan, pushing its market cap above 400 billion yuan. But in 2025, it generated just 1.644 billion yuan in revenue and posted a net loss of 789 million yuan. The lofty valuations of China’s homegrown GPU companies are entering the performance-validation stage.

Third, OpenAI, Meta, and SpaceX’s xAI are all competing to build more cost-efficient models. The focus is shifting from simply pushing the limits of capability to seeing who can train and run models at lower cost.

Meanwhile, Apple is already planning AI capabilities across its M6, M7, and M8 chips—signaling that on-device AI will become even more closely tied to hardware upgrade cycles.

So when evaluating AI investments from here, don’t just look at benchmarks and stories. Watch three things: whether capital is broadening out, whether profits can materialize, and whether each model call can actually make money.
