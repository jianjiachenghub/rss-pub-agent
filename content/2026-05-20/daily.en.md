# Personal Daily Report | May 20, 2026

## Today's Take

> The AI industry is undergoing a phase transition from "unlimited investment" to "calculated investment." Google's opening of TPUs to external users and Alibaba's release of self-developed chips signal a shift in the compute landscape from monopoly to tiered competition; yet the "impossible math" and surging data center credit simultaneously warn of a ceiling for capital intensity. More critically, geopolitical conflicts have materially transmitted to monetary policy—the ECB's rate hike risks and Nigeria's predicament indicate tightening global liquidity, while Meta's mass layoffs reveal tech companies attempting to hedge financial uncertainty through organizational downsizing. The hedging tension between compute expansion and financial tightening is today's true main thread.
