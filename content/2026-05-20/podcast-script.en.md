**"Personal Daily" Podcast Script**

**Hosts:** A (Male voice, macro perspective) / B (Female voice, tech and business details)

---

**A:** Morning. There's a lot to unpack today, but let's zero in on the main thread: AI compute infrastructure is undergoing a structural reshuffle.

**B:** Right. Compute used to be a closely guarded competitive moat, but now it's become an openly contested strategic resource.

**A:** The clearest example is the Google-Blackstone news. Google is opening up its TPU chips at scale—no longer just for internal use. They're spinning up an independent company with Blackstone, which is putting up $5 billion to stand up 500 megawatts of compute capacity by 2027.

**B:** This is a critical shift. Training large models used to mean NVIDIA GPUs were basically your only option. Now Google TPUs are entering as an independent service provider, creating an alternative path for companies outside the NVIDIA ecosystem. Plus, this partnership model—tech giants bringing the technology, financial giants bringing the capital—could become the standard template for heavy infrastructure investments down the road.

**A:** Meanwhile, self-sufficiency is another obvious through-line. Alibaba just released a new self-developed AI chip that handles both training and inference. The objective is clear-cut: cut reliance on external supply chains.

**B:** Europe's on the same track. French firms are bidding on a 10 billion euro project to build Europe's own AI super-factory. When you look at Google's TPU opening, China's homegrown chips, and Europe's super-factory all together, it shows compute has moved beyond tech competition into geopolitical infrastructure warfare.

**A:** This spending frenzy has side effects, though. Wall Street's getting jittery because debt for building AI data centers is ballooning too fast—it's already on the risk radar. There are concerns about over-leverage and credit concentration.

**B:** Plus, we're about to find out just how big AI's appetite really is. NVIDIA's earnings this week will be the litmus test. If guidance disappoints, the chip sector rally might hit pause. After all, the infrastructure build-out has had a "build first, ask questions later" vibe to it.

**A:** While CapEx is exploding, the org chart is actually shrinking. Meta's cutting 8,000 jobs globally—squeezing resources out of non-core units to pour everything into AI.

**B:** But this "all in AI" play is running into friction on the ground. Every bank CEO wants AI efficiency gains, but regulators are warning that data privacy, consumer protection, and financial stability risks have to be factored in. Going forward, compliance costs will directly determine the speed and cost structure of AI deployment. Banks need to find a new balance between innovation and regulation.

**A:** Geopolitics is warping monetary policy too. ECB member Wunsch made it clear: if the war continues, a June rate hike is likely. The Iran situation isn't just boosting oil prices—it's feeding imported inflation into emerging markets like Nigeria and South Africa, leaving their central banks caught in the middle.

**B:** On the technical side, there's a neat little project called "oh-my-pi"—an AI coding agent. It doesn't just do code completion; it acts like an intern, proactively understanding tasks, operating browsers, checking docs, and independently completing coding assignments.

**A:** This is the shift from "AI-assisted" to "AI agent"—it could fundamentally reshape software development workflows.

**B:** One final data point: AI search company Exa just hit a $2.2 billion valuation, with a16z leading the round. Even with giants like Google dominating search, AI-native companies are still commanding premium valuations. That tells you the market believes AI is going to redefine what search means.

**A:** Exactly. From compute infrastructure to the application layer, from capital euphoria to regulatory tightening—today's picture is clear: AI is evolving from a lab technology into heavy-asset, highly-regulated, geopolitical core infrastructure. See you tomorrow.

**B:** See you tomorrow.
