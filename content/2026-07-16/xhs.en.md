Today’s Must-Read: AI Goes Real, Capital Shifts to Energy

July 16 brought a flood of developments. Today, it’s not just AI models that matter. More importantly, war is reshaping interest rates in Asia, computing demand is transforming energy investment, and on-device AI is finally approaching real-world deployment.

💰 Macro & Markets: Inflationary Pressure Is Rising Again

The Bank of Korea announced an interest-rate hike in response to higher energy prices and inflation driven by the war in Iran. Meanwhile, markets are focused on how the Federal Reserve will reduce its roughly $7 trillion balance sheet and whether it will adjust its policy framework.

Another potential variable is China’s crude oil demand. If imports recover, the already limited buffer in global oil markets could shrink further, making oil-price volatility worth watching closely.

🤖 AI: Competition Is Shifting from Model Capabilities to Product Gateways

Moonshot is preparing to launch a new model aimed directly at Anthropic, while Thinking Machines, founded by Mira Murati, drew on technical approaches developed by Chinese teams for its first model. The technological paths of Chinese and U.S. foundation models are rapidly converging.

Closer to everyday users, seven on-device AI services—including Apple Intelligence—have completed regulatory registration, with Qwen set to provide the underlying capabilities for the China-market version. On-device AI has gained independent regulatory status for the first time, reducing uncertainty around the launch of Apple Intelligence in mainland China.

At the same time, China is stepping up oversight of “human-AI romance” and designs that foster emotional dependence. Companion AI will need to address not only content, but also anthropomorphism, retention mechanisms, and protections for minors.

⚡ Energy & Capital: The AI Boom Is Spreading Upstream

Energy-sector IPOs are rising noticeably. The reason is straightforward: as more data centers are built, electricity is becoming a critical constraint on AI expansion.

This means AI investment is moving beyond chips and models toward supply-side infrastructure such as power generation, grids, and energy storage.

📉 Company Updates: Even Star Assets Face Valuation Tests

SpaceX shares fell below their IPO price of $135 for the first time, signaling that the post-listing premium is beginning to fade.

Meanwhile, Warren Buffett confirmed that he personally approved an increase in Berkshire Hathaway’s Alphabet stake, now worth nearly $21 billion. Value investors are reassessing the long-term odds of AI platforms, but the ultimate test remains whether massive R&D spending can translate into real revenue.

🛠️ Developers & Industry: AI Enters the Governance and Mass-Manufacturing Stage

Anaconda acquired Kilo Code in a bid to unify AI coding, security governance, and development environments. Apache Ossie, meanwhile, is attempting to establish a shared semantic layer across AI, BI, and analytics platforms to reduce inconsistent metrics and duplicated modeling.

In the physical economy, XPeng plans to raise monthly robot production to more than 1,000 units. Embodied AI is moving beyond showcase prototypes toward proving its ability to scale manufacturing.

In one sentence: Today’s clearest trend is that AI is moving beyond a pure model race and into smartphones, energy, developer governance, and robot manufacturing—while war, oil prices, and monetary policy are once again becoming major forces shaping asset prices.
