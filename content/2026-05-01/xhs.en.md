⚔️ **The Pentagon’s AI Contracts Go To…**
- The U.S. Department of Defense signed new AI procurement deals—Nvidia, Microsoft, and Amazon scored big. Anthropic’s Claude model was excluded due to compliance issues. **The military AI supply chain is increasingly concentrated in the hands of major players**. 🛡️

- Anthropic wanted to expand access to a model called Mythos, which is reportedly capable of launching cyberattacks. But the White House objected on security grounds. **AI safety regulation is tightening—not every model can be played with freely**. 🛫

📱 **Why Is OpenAI Making a Phone? Insiders Spilled the Beans Behind Closed Doors.**
- It’s not just about making an AI assistant app! Their hardware lead says it’s about **regaining fundamental control over AI operations**—they’ve even developed their own chips (tape-out in just two years, impressive). 💸 **ChatGPT Might Get Cheaper, but with Ads!**
- OpenAI plans to introduce a cheaper ChatGPT tier, subsidized by ads—similar to the Netflix model. **AI services could shift from pure subscription to a hybrid model: free/cheap with ads + paid ad-free**. Your attention just became more valuable.

🇨🇳 **Nvidia Faces Hurdles, Huawei AI Chips Are Selling Like Crazy!**
- With restricted supply of Nvidia’s high-end chips in China, domestic automakers, security firms, and others are switching en masse to Huawei’s Ascend AI processors. **Domestic high-end AI computing substitution has entered the stage of large-scale practical deployment**.

🔧 **GitHub Copilot CLI Two Modes, Programmers Rejoice**
- GitHub Copilot’s command-line tool now offers two modes: interactive (like chatting, with context memory) and non-interactive (one-off commands). **Developer efficiency is about to get another boost**, especially for tasks requiring sequential operations.

This signals AI is no longer just a functional module but set to become the core engine driving the community, e-commerce, and commercialization.
