**Today's Assessment**: The bottleneck in AI development is shifting from chips to energy and supply chains, accelerating industry fragmentation.

**Key Signals**:
1.  **Shifting Cost Structure**: 90% of Nvidia's production costs rely on Asian suppliers, intensifying competition for capacity. Surging AI power demands have pushed U.S. electricity capital expenditure to $78 billion.
2.  **Deepening Value Chain**: Samsung's market cap surpasses $1 trillion on AI memory chip demand, shifting market focus from training chips to memory and infrastructure.
3.  **Product Form Evolution**: Meta is developing "autonomous" AI assistants capable of proactively executing tasks, signaling a move from chatbots to intelligent agents.

**What to Watch Next**: With high costs, who will bear the expense of AI commercialization, and when will profitable business models become clear.
