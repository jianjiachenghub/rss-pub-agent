**Podcast Script: Personal Daily**

**Total Runtime: Approx. 4 minutes 50 seconds**

---

**[Opening]**

**A:** Welcome back. Today, there's one major thread worth watching closely: computing power is rapidly transforming from a technical resource into a foundational financial asset.

**B:** Right. It feels like oil or electricity back in the day—starting as industrial raw materials, then becoming core tradable and hedgeable assets in financial markets.

**A:** Exactly. From the New York Stock Exchange to energy giants, from chip tycoons to the Vatican, everyone is recalibrating around this shift. Let's pick a few key stories to discuss.

---

**[Item 1: Compute Financialization]**

**B:** Let's start with the most obvious one. ICE, the parent company of the New York Stock Exchange—Intercontinental Exchange—is planning to launch a **compute futures market**.

**A:** That's a strong signal. Previously, when companies rented GPUs or bought computing power, prices followed market rates and they bore all the risk themselves. Now ICE wants to standardize it into tradable futures contracts.

**B:** Simply put, they're installing price discovery and risk hedging tools for the core resource of AI infrastructure. Going forward, computing power prices could become a barometer for AI industry sentiment, just like crude oil prices.

---

**[Item 2: Energy Infrastructure]**

**A:** Behind computing power is electricity. America's largest utility company, NextEra, is pushing forward an **$118 billion** deal specifically to address the explosive power demands of AI data centers.

**B:** That scale is mind-blowing—roughly the GDP of some small nations. This shows AI isn't just competition at the software level; it's already scrambling to lock down physical energy infrastructure.

**A:** Right. Electricity is becoming a hard constraint on AI expansion. Whoever controls cheap power and stable data centers gains the upper hand in the next-generation computing race.

---

**[Item 3: Tech Giants' High-Stakes Bet]**

**B:** Speaking of big bets, Meta demonstrated today what it means to leave no way out. They're advancing a **$200 billion** AI data center construction plan.

**A:** This money backs their artificial general intelligence vision. But honestly, it's essentially betting the company's future, gambling that this astronomical investment will eventually pay off through AI operations.

**B:** Meanwhile, they're doing a massive internal reshuffle, reassigning seven thousand employees to AI-related positions. Look—on one side you have historic capital expenditures, on the other a complete reallocation of human resources. Meta is dead set on going all in.

---

**[Item 4: Geopolitics and Chip Controls]**

**A:** However, hardware competition is drawing more regulatory pressure. NVIDIA CEO Jensen Huang recently went public at Stanford, openly opposing AI chip export controls.

**B:** He fiercely rejected comparisons of GPUs to nuclear weapons, calling that analogy absurd. He argues that controls are not only ineffective but actually counterproductive, damaging American industry itself.

**A:** This reveals the tension between top chipmakers and geopolitics. For markets, this means expectations for global chip supply chain stability are shifting. The costs of technological nationalism may ultimately be borne by the entire industry.

---

**[Item 5: AI's Real Impact on Employment]**

**B:** Moving from infrastructure and geopolitics to the ground level. AI's impact on the labor market is no longer theoretical. Standard Chartered plans to cut **15%** of back-office positions; the UK's NHS is also using AI to downsize hiring and avoid fiscal collapse.

**A:** Even AustralianSuper, Australia's largest pension fund, is warning that Agentic AI capable of autonomous decision-making will disrupt the asset management industry.

**B:** These signals are clear: AI is shifting from assistive tool to replacement solution, especially in highly structured fields like finance and healthcare. Job restructuring may happen faster than expected.

---

**[Item 6: Macro Market Environment]**

**A:** Finally, a look at the macro environment. US 30-year Treasury yields hit a 20-year high, and global markets are deeply divided on this.

**B:** This interest rate uncertainty is also affecting hot money in AI investments. While AI-themed stocks are still rising, other high-risk assets are feeling the pressure. Capital is becoming more selective; it's no longer a rising-tide-lifts-all-boats market.

**A:** Goldman Sachs remains optimistic, saying this volatility creates huge opportunities for trading desks. But either way, interest rate levels are becoming the critical valve determining whether AI capital expenditures can continue.

---

**[Closing]**

**B:** One final interesting contrast. There's also news today that Pope Francis and Anthropic co-founder Amodei will jointly release an encyclical on AI ethics.

**A:** From Wall Street's compute futures to the Vatican's ethical encyclical, AI has simultaneously become a trading vehicle for financial engineers and a philosophical issue for religious leaders. This breadth itself shows that what we're experiencing isn't just technological change, but a comprehensive restructuring of social infrastructure.

**B:** Alright, that's it for today. Stay tuned, see you tomorrow.

**A:** See you tomorrow.
