**Podcast Script: Personal Daily**

**Hosts:** A (Focus on Technology and Industry Perspective), B (Focus on Market and Macro Perspective)
**Total Duration:** Approximately 5 minutes

---

**[Opening]**

**A:** Hello everyone, welcome to today's Personal Daily podcast. I'm A.

**B:** I'm B. Today's main storyline has truly been long-awaited—the Chinese version of Apple Intelligence has finally landed.

**A:** That's right, this is definitely the most noteworthy event today. As of March 31st, eligible iPhone users can already see it. The big question on everyone's mind—"what they've actually been cooking up"—has also been revealed. You activate it by long-pressing the power button, but tests suggest the large language model on the backend is calling Baidu's Ernie Bot, while the visual intelligence uses Google's engine.

**B:** That combination is quite interesting. Before, everyone was guessing who Apple would choose as a partner. Now it looks like Apple has taken a hybrid path of "local large model plus global service components."

**A:** Exactly. This is a landmark event for domestic large models. It means the battlefield for commercialization has upgraded from simple API calls to a war for "terminal pre-installation" entry points. Baidu has effectively secured a top-tier consumer electronics endorsement this time, but how the regulatory boundaries are drawn later remains a key variable.

---

**[Segment One: AI Frontiers and Challenges]**

**B:** Speaking of large models, besides the news from Apple, DeepSeek also ran into a situation yesterday. They experienced their most severe outage since going live—the web client and app were down for a full seven hours.

**A:** Seven hours is indeed no short amount of time. Although officials said the old model API wasn't affected, this is a wake-up call for developers. The traffic surge brought by free strategies, combined with rapid model iteration, has exposed the shortcomings in infrastructure stability quite clearly.

**B:** It really is. If your business is entirely betting on a single model, this kind of continuity risk is very real. However, I heard that during the recovery period, they seemingly updated the web model as well; text quality and SVG generation capabilities appear to have improved.

**A:** That’s pretty intense—talk about a grind. On another note, Alibaba Qianwen also released something new today, launching the omni-modal large model Qwen3.5-Omni. It supports 256k context and can process over 10 hours of audio. For the open-source ecosystem, omni-modal and long context are now becoming standard features.

---

**[Segment Two: Developers and Career Reflections]**

**B:** Tools are getting easier to use, but the requirements for people are changing too. I saw a viewpoint from a QCon London speech that was very sharp, saying that AI is eating away the "middle rungs" of engineer growth.

**A:** That metaphor is very vivid. The basic tasks engineers used to do in their first ten years were exactly the process for cultivating judgment and intuition. Now that AI can perfectly handle these junior tasks, how do new recruits grow into senior engineers?

**B:** Exactly. This forces the threshold for career entry points to be raised. If enterprises don't redesign their training mechanisms, they might face a gap in mid-to-senior level talent in the future. This isn't just an education issue; it's a management challenge.

**A:** However, on the tool level, things are becoming more interoperable. OpenAI even made a plugin for their Codex that allows the competitor Claude Code to call it. It’s much smoother for developers to switch between different AI assistants now, and the speed of workflow integration is accelerating.

---

**[Segment Three: Markets and Investment Logic]**

**B:** Let's shift our gaze to the market. Recently, a Google paper was accused of academic misconduct by Chinese scholars, causing quite a stir in investment circles. That TurboQuant paper previously claimed to drastically compress memory, and when it was released, it triggered a slump in memory stocks.

**A:** Now it's being accused of plagiarism, and it's heading to a top conference without corrections. This actually shakes a certain logic: are we relying too much on single papers to make technical investment judgments?

**B:** Indeed. The disconnect between top conference peer reviews and the industry's rapid response is just too severe. When investors bet on technical routes in the future, they might have to factor review risks into the cost.

**A:** Looking over at the Hong Kong stock market, capital is still crazy. For the new chip stock Fourier, the IPO was oversubscribed by over 3,000 times, with subscription amounts nearing 100 billion Hong Kong dollars.

**B:** 3,000 times—that is simply fanaticism. Driven by both geopolitics and AI demand, hard tech assets are currently a capital consensus, and the scarcity of chips has been amplified to the extreme.

---

**[Segment Four: Macro and Geopolitics]**

**A:** Finally, let's talk macro. The situation in the Middle East is tense again. Iran attacked a fully loaded Kuwaiti oil tanker, and oil prices rose in response, with US crude closing above 100 dollars.

**B:** The S&P 500 is also extending its decline. This indicates that geopolitical risk premium has been officially priced into assets. The impact of supply chain fragility on inflation, along with the squeeze of energy costs on corporate profits, will likely need to be re-evaluated in the second half of the year.

**A:** Meanwhile, over at the Fed, New York Fed President Williams said current interest rates are "just right," maintaining a patient stance. It looks like we'll have to wait a bit longer for rate cuts.

---

**[Closing]**

**B:** There's one other small development worth noting. The State Administration for Market Regulation has proposed studying and formulating "Regulations on Prohibiting Unfair Competition on the Internet." In the future, data acquisition and algorithm recommendations by AI companies may face more systematic compliance reviews.

**A:** Compliance is indeed a trend. Alright, that wraps up today's selected daily report. Whether it's the landing of Apple Intelligence or the missing rungs for engineers, both are worth our continued attention.

**B:** Thanks for listening, and we'll see you in the next episode.
