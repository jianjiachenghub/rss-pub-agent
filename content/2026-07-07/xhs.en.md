**Tech Is Getting Pretty Hardcore Today**

AI is still the main thread, but the most interesting part today is this: chips, capital, financial risk, defense tech, and office automation are all getting pulled into the same wave.

1. **Domestic AI Chips Are Heating Up**

DeepSeek is reportedly developing its own AI chips, and more Chinese companies are turning to domestic chip suppliers.

This is not just about “replacing Nvidia.” The real shift is that AI companies will compete not only on model performance, but also on whether they can secure stable compute, bring down inference costs, and make hardware and software work better together.

2. **AI Investment Enters the Trillion-Dollar Era**

Amazon plans to issue at least $25 billion in bonds to keep investing in AI infrastructure. Meanwhile, AI capital expenditure by the five major tech giants in 2026 is expected to exceed $800 billion, and may even surpass U.S. defense spending.

This shows that AI is no longer just a hot sector. It is now affecting the macroeconomy, bond markets, rate expectations, and the capital goods cycle.

3. **Samsung Keeps Benefiting from the AI Boom**

Samsung posted record profits for the third consecutive quarter, driven by AI demand for high-end memory and semiconductors.

In simple terms, AI is not only making money for model companies. It is also continuing to lift upstream chips, memory, servers, and the broader supply chain.

4. **Financial Regulators Are Watching AI Attacks Closely**

Banking regulators are warning about the risks of AI-driven cyberattacks. Future attacks on financial institutions will become more automated and more complex.

AI security is no longer just an issue inside the tech industry. It has entered the financial regulatory agenda.

5. **U.S. Inflation Expectations Are Rising Again**

A New York Fed survey shows that U.S. consumers’ inflation expectations rose in June, especially for healthcare and rent.

This will affect market expectations around Fed rate cuts. If inflation data does not cooperate later, the rate-cut trade may not go so smoothly.

6. **Defense Tech Becomes a New VC Favorite**

Aerospace and military-related startups are being embraced by Silicon Valley again, with investment doubling.

Capital is shifting from pure software growth stories toward policy orders, geopolitical security, and the certainty of hard tech.

7. **AI Office Automation Keeps Moving Forward**

OfficeCLI is gaining attention on GitHub. It lets AI agents read and write Word, Excel, and PPT files without installing Office.

Tools like this matter because the entry point for AI agents is moving from chat windows into real office workflows.

8. **Tencent Sells Kuaishou Stake and Doubles Down on AI Transformation**

Tencent sold $1.5 billion worth of Kuaishou shares, sending a signal that major tech companies are moving resources away from mature internet assets and toward new growth in AI, cloud, and content ecosystems.

**One-sentence summary:**

The key point today is not that “AI is hot again,” but that AI is reshuffling chip supply chains, big tech capital spending, financial regulation, defense investment, and office software all at once. What really matters is this: whoever can turn massive investment into stable revenue, and whoever can control reliable compute, is more likely to gain the upper hand in the next stage.
