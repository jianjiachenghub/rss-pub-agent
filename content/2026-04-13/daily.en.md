**Event:** The article analyzes how AI tools boost individual productivity 10x, yet enterprise value sees no corresponding growth—drawing analogy to the 19th-century electricity revolution: textile mills saw no output growth for 30 years after switching to electric motors, until factories completely redesigned assembly lines and division of labor to realize returns.

**Why it matters:** This reveals the threshold for engineering efficiency leaps: simply introducing AI tools doesn't automatically translate to enterprise-level gains; organizational architecture and workflows must be redesigned in parallel.
