**"Personal Daily" Podcast Script: Two-Person Dialogue Version**

**Hosts:** A (Macro/Market Perspective), B (Tech/Industry Perspective)

---

**A:** Morning. Today's market moves are quite interesting.

**B:** Yeah, the Dow hit another record high, but meanwhile the Fed is warning about war risks. This contrast feels like the market and the central bank are playing tug-of-war.

**A:** Right, look at the S&P and Nasdaq both up over 1%, completely ignoring the tensions in the Middle East. But Fed Governor Waller came right out and said that if the Iran conflict escalates and drives oil prices higher, it could trigger a "sustained price shock."

**B:** That means inflation could flare up again, raising the bar for Fed rate cuts. Everyone was discussing how many cuts we'd get this year; now we might need to reconsider, or even... hike rates?

**A:** Exactly. This changes the entire interest rate path expectations. Global asset pricing has to adjust accordingly.

**B:** By contrast, the ECB is much more dovish. Koch explicitly said not to preemptively hike rates just because of uncertainty.

**A:** The ECB is determined to wait and see; at least in the short term, they won't pivot hawkish in sync with the Fed.

**B:** Turning to tech, the flow of money and hype is clear. Recursive, founded just months ago, raised $500 million at a $4 billion valuation.

**A:** Built by former DeepMind and OpenAI people, focused on "self-learning" AI to reduce reliance on labeled data.

**B:** Right. Capital is clearly hunting for the next technical path beyond Transformers. And OpenAI is also securing compute power—heard they're spending $20 billion on Cerebras chips and taking equity.

**A:** This shifts from simple procurement to deep integration, securing inference compute supply for the coming years while also giving Cerebras's IPO an endorsement.

**B:** Anthropic isn't sitting idle either. They launched Claude Design, which sent Figma's stock down nearly 7%. The market sees that AI-native tools are challenging the competitive moats of traditional design software.

**A:** OpenAI isn't just pushing into design, but drug discovery too. The new project is called GPT-Rosalind; big pharma like Amgen and Moderna are already using it. This is typical of AI companies finding new revenue curves in high-value vertical industries.

**B:** Meanwhile, Nvidia released a new OCR model trained entirely on synthetic data. If they can solve the real data bottleneck, it changes the cost structure of AI development.

**A:** Back to geopolitics. Trump recently hinted the Iran war is winding down, shifting focus back to the economy. In an election year, that sounds like a clear signal.

**B:** The market is indeed welcoming this de-escalation. Look at Hungary—expectations of a new government pursuing pro-EU reforms have stocks, bonds, and currency all rallying.

**A:** But the Bulgarian election results tell a different story. Pro-Russian factions are leading, called a "Trojan horse" by foreign media. This shows political risks in Eastern Europe are diverging; pro-Russian forces taking power could fracture the EU's Russia policy.

**B:** Finally, looking at Asia, Qunhe Technology—one of the "Hangzhou Six Little Dragons"—listed in Hong Kong, jumping 170% at the open. In the home design SaaS vertical, companies with clear business models and technical moats can still command high valuations even in cautious market conditions.

**A:** Today's market seems to be finding balance among multiple narratives—war and peace, inflation and rate cuts, general-purpose models and vertical applications.

**B:** Right. Every variable is dynamically adjusting. We'll keep watching. See you tomorrow.
