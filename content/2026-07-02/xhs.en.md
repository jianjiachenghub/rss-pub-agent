Title: Today’s Must-Read: AI, Jobs, and Markets Are All Changing

Body:

There’s a lot to absorb today, and not just in AI. The real point to watch is this: AI is continuing to become “national-level infrastructure,” while jobs, interest rates, energy, and capital markets are all being repriced.

1. U.S. jobs data shapes the Fed’s thinking  
The U.S. unemployment rate edged down in June, but hiring growth is slowing. Markets are now caught in a bind: employment is still holding up, giving the Fed reason to stay relatively hawkish; at the same time, growth momentum is weakening, which pushed Treasury prices higher.

Simply put: rate-cut expectations are no longer that solid.

2. AI companies are starting to look more like strategic infrastructure  
OpenAI was reportedly advised to offer the U.S. government a 5% equity stake in exchange for policy and resource support within a broader AI initiative. That signal matters: leading AI companies may no longer be just commercial businesses, but deeply tied to national compute capacity, regulation, and government procurement.

The White House is also accelerating work on AI model standards. In the future, model capabilities, safety boundaries, and application rules will become more institutionalized.

3. The AI compute war has entered a new phase  
Anthropic is reportedly in talks with Samsung on custom AI chips. SoftBank plans to provide 10-gigawatt-scale AI computing services in the U.S. Kling AI raised $2 billion to expand AI video.

But on the other side, Meta may shift from being a buyer of compute to a seller of compute, directly challenging the narrative that “GPUs will always be in short supply.” Stocks linked to storage, chips, and independent AI clouds have seen major volatility.

4. AI investing is starting to diverge  
Investors are using leveraged ETFs to accelerate bets on AI, and bullish trades on the Nasdaq 100 are heating up. But a sharp drop in Korean AI stocks weighed on emerging markets, while financing tied to Meta and SoftBank also exposed divergence in the AI debt market.

The key point: markets no longer buy anything just because it has “AI” attached. Cash flow, customer quality, and asset-backed financing capacity will matter more.

5. AI is changing jobs and corporate spending  
The finance and tech sectors are reportedly cutting around 28,000 jobs per month. Microsoft is reallocating 6,000 employees to help customers adopt AI. SAP is cutting hiring and travel, and putting the money into AI.

This shows AI is no longer just a “trial tool.” It is reshaping corporate budgets, job structures, and organizational priorities.

6. Developer tools are also reshuffling entry points  
openai/codex-plugin-cc is gaining traction on GitHub, allowing Claude Code to call Codex for code reviews and task delegation. Kimi K2.7 Code has entered GitHub Copilot, marking Copilot’s first inclusion of an open-weight model.

In the future, developers may not rely on just one AI tool, but instead combine calls across IDEs, agents, and models.

7. A few non-AI points also matter  
Denmark’s central bank intervened in the krone exchange rate. A Bank of England official said they are ready to raise rates if necessary. Blue Owl is facing $4.7 billion in redemption pressure. On energy, U.S. natural gas may surpass oil as the top energy source by 2030, which is also tied to power demand from AI data centers.

Today’s one-sentence summary:

The AI theme is still intact, but the market is moving from “storytelling” to “doing the math.” Jobs data, rate expectations, compute supply, and corporate cash flow will decide who keeps rising in the next round, and who gets squeezed out first.
