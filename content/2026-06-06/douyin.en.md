(Hook) When Nvidia releases a new AI model and SpaceX goes public with a high-profile IPO, why did U.S. chip stocks plummet by over a thousand points?

As a tech blogger, my most important takeaway today is: **The market's grand narrative around AI is shifting from hype over capital expenditures to scrutiny of real performance delivery.** Valuation anchors for tech stocks might be resetting.

The most direct signal is last night's sharp sell-off in the Nasdaq, which plunged over 1,100 points, led by chip stocks. The core reason is growing market skepticism about whether the massive, hundred-billion-dollar AI infrastructure investments can be sustained, coupled with concerns over the interest rate outlook.

But on the very same day, Nvidia made a heavyweight move by launching the world's first full-scale open-source physical AI model. This isn’t just an incremental upgrade—it marks a new phase of real-world implementation. By spearheading a global alliance, Nvidia is signaling it doesn’t just want to sell computing power, but to deeply embed itself in the foundational technical standards of next-generation AI.

Simultaneously, major news broke about SpaceX’s IPO, with reports indicating the offering price will be set firmly at $135 per share, with no changes. At a time of volatility in global capital markets, Musk’s choice to enter with a fixed and relatively high price is itself a key test of current market risk appetite.

So you see, on one side, Wall Street is reevaluating the valuation logic for tech stocks, while on the other, tech giants continue to charge ahead, carving out new arenas. The tension between these two forces will shape where we find opportunities—and landmines—moving forward.
