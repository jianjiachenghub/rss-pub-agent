Combined with concerns about rising interest rates, the valuation anchors for tech stocks are clearly loosening.

**[Nvidia Open-Sources Physics Model]**

**B:** But interestingly, right amid this pessimistic sentiment, Nvidia announced Cosmos 3 at the GTC conference.

**A:** Right, this "omnimodal physics AI model" is pretty crucial. It's not just about seeing and hearing—it connects visual reasoning, world generation, and action prediction. Nvidia is also leading a global alliance, and **the signal is clear**—they want to own the infrastructure layer for physical AI. Even if market sentiment fluctuates, the technical layout can't stop.

**[SpaceX IPO Pricing]**

**B:** Another capital market focus is SpaceX. I heard the IPO price is locked in at $135 and won't budge?

**A:** Yeah, it's set in stone. This is quite telling in the current environment—it's both a test for high-risk appetite investors and reflects management's confidence in their valuation. After all, Starship just had a successful test flight, and this pricing signal will affect the IPO pace of the hard-tech sector in the coming weeks.

**[Tencent's AI Strategic Pivot]**

**B:** Looking beyond international news, Tencent domestically has also sent strategic signals. Yao Shunyu talked about building a "long-term AGI organization."

**A:** This formulation is worth pondering. It's no longer just about competing on model parameters, but emphasizing **organizational capability accumulation**. The three directions are also quite pragmatic—pre-training and post-training will continue, but the focus is clearly shifting toward productization and new research paradigms. Put plainly, the big tech companies are starting to seriously consider ROI; they can't burn money indefinitely.

**[Agent Search Infrastructure]**

**B:** There's also new movement on the technical infrastructure front. Exa's CEO said something interesting on the a16z podcast—he said the search that Agents need is fundamentally different from traditional search.

**A:** Right, he said we need to shift from "humans seeking information" to "systems autonomously acquiring it," with search moving from reactive querying to **proactive context presetting**. What does this mean? The traditional search advertising business model might be bypassed by Agents connecting directly to data sources. What Exa wants to capture is the default choice for this new pipeline entry point.

**[Open Source Community Governance Crisis]**

**B:** Speaking of AI's side effects, that whole Ladybird browser incident is pretty thought-provoking.

**A:** Yeah, they were forced to announce **they're stopping acceptance of public Pull Requests**. Because AI code generation tools have become so widespread, there's been a flood of low-quality, even security-vulnerable automated submissions, and maintainers simply can't review them all.

**B:** Is this a crisis for the traditional open source collaboration model?

**A:** You could say that. AI has raised the governance costs of "openness," and the boundaries between core developers and peripheral contributors are hardening. The open source community may need to rethink how to maintain code quality under the impact of AI automation.

**[Developer Tool Layer Updates]**

**B:** Finally, let's look at the tooling layer. Aifei claims to be the world's first AI Coding framework, redesigning Java development from the structural level itself. And then there's MateClaw 1.5.0, which focused on adding foundational team collaboration capabilities like Goal Checklists and memory isolation.

**A:** Looking at these signals together, they're quite consistent—whether it's big tech strategy, infrastructure, or developer tools, AI is shifting from "flexing muscles" to **"cultivating internal strength."** Solving hard problems like real team collaboration, code acceptance, and system architecture—that's the main theme of the second half.

**A:** You could summarize it that way. The market is voting with stock prices, technology is iterating through frameworks, and the open source community is adjusting through rules—everyone is simultaneously answering one question: how exactly should AI be grounded in reality.
