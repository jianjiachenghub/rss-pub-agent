## AI

### NVIDIA Announces World's First Fully Open Fully Multimodal Physical AI Model, NVIDIA Cosmos 3

**Event:** At the 2026 Taipei GTC conference, NVIDIA announced NVIDIA Cosmos 3, an open-world foundation model for physical AI. Leveraging a novel hybrid Transformer architecture, the model unifies three core capabilities—visual reasoning, world generation, and action prediction—becoming the world's first fully open fully multimodal physical AI model. Concurrently, NVIDIA established the NVIDIA Cosmos Coalition, a global collaboration alliance uniting top world-model research teams and AI developers to drive the technical iteration and widespread adoption of next-generation open-world models, heralding the accelerated arrival of large-scale physical AI deployment.

**Why it matters:** NVIDIA's new AI model release signals a significant shift in AI capability paradigms, with future implications for AI platform dynamics and chip demand.

### Tencent's Yao Shunyu: China Needs Long-Term AGI Organizations, AI Business Focuses on Three Directions

**Event:** At the Tencent AI Industry Summit, Tencent Chief AI Scientist Yao Shunyu stated that China's core task for the next phase of AI is building long-term AGI organizations. He outlined three strategic directions for Tencent AI: continuously optimizing pre-training and post-training foundations; translating core technologies into practical products that create societal value; and actively exploring new research paradigms to uncover emerging opportunities.

**Why it matters:** Tencent's AI strategy is shifting from model training competitions toward organizational capability accumulation and commercialization efficiency. While pre-training and post-training remain prioritized—indicating continued investment in foundation model iteration—the "long-term AGI organization" framing suggests major tech firms are now balancing research cycles with commercial returns. AI productization capabilities will become the critical variable in next-stage competition.

### Exa CEO on AI Agent Search Architecture: Traditional Search Engines Cannot Meet Autonomous System Demands

**Event:** On the a16z podcast, Exa CEO Will Bryk detailed the logic behind building search infrastructure for AI Agents, noting that traditional search engines' ranking mechanisms—based on human click behavior—cannot satisfy autonomous systems' real-time decision-making requirements. He emphasized that retrieval in Agent workflows must shift from reactive querying to proactive context presetting, predicting that search will become the foundational layer protocol of the Agent economy.

**Why it matters:** Agent search is transitioning from "humans seeking information" to "systems autonomously acquiring information," requiring the retrieval layer to evolve from user-facing page indexes to API-callable structured data interfaces. AI-native search companies like Exa are competing for default data pipeline entry and distribution rights in the Agent economy, while traditional search advertising business models face obsolescence risk as Agents bypass intermediaries to connect directly with data sources.

### Ladybird Browser Shuts Down Public Pull Requests Due to Flood of AI-Generated Code

![Ladybird Browser Shuts Down Public Pull Requests Due to Flood of AI-Generated Code](https://supertechfans.com/cn/post/2026-06-06-HackerNews/)

**Event:** The maintainers of the open-source browser project Ladybird announced the termination of their public Pull Request collaboration model, citing an overwhelming influx of low-quality, automated submissions—including some containing security vulnerabilities—generated by AI coding tools. This flood severely threatened code base quality and overwhelmed maintainer review capacity, forcing the project to pivot to an inner-source development model accepting submissions only from core contributors.

**Why it matters:** The proliferation of AI code generation tools is raising governance costs and review barriers for open-source projects. The traditional "open to all" collaborative model faces obsolescence risks. Project review cycles and maintenance mechanisms require reconstruction to combat automated spam, while boundaries between core and peripheral contributors will harden—potentially altering open-source software evolution velocity and community ecosystem structures.
