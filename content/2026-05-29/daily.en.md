# Daily Briefing | May 29, 2026

## Today's Take

> The deep coupling of state capital and frontier models is accelerating. The National Integrated Circuit Industry Investment Fund's lead investment in DeepSeek signals the AI race is entering a "national team" phase. OpenAI's biodefense initiative shows model capabilities penetrating critical infrastructure, while a six-month compute leasing agreement hints at resource scarcity. Combined with IBM's $5 billion bet on open-source security, the logic for building sovereign AI infrastructure is now clear. Valuation frameworks are being reconfigured in tandem; the billion-dollar exit threshold is obsolete, and the speed of scaling has replaced scale itself as the core metric.

## Business

### Musk: SpaceX's Colossus AI Lease Agreement with Anthropic is Only for Six Months

**Event:** SpaceX CEO Elon Musk said Thursday that SpaceX has only agreed to lease its Colossus AI training data center cluster to Anthropic for six months, though he added the deal "could potentially" be extended for years. Earlier this year, SpaceX struck a deal for Anthropic to pay $1.25 billion a month to use the computing power of the Colossus and Colossus II data center clusters in Memphis, Tennessee, through May 2029. Musk stated the deal is a 180-day lease, after which either party can terminate with 90 days' notice. "We won’t leave them in the lurch and will provide a reasonable exit, but if compute becomes extremely tight, I have represented that we may need to pull it back at some point," he said.

**Why it matters:** Musk's clarification on the details of the high-value compute lease provides hard data on AI infrastructure costs and planning.

## Investing & Finance

### a16z & VenCap: AI Companies Scale Faster Than Traditional Startups, Making Billion-Dollar Exits an Outdated Benchmark

**Event:** In a podcast, a16z partner David George and VenCap partner David Clark analyzed the structural impact of AI on venture capital and the tech market. They noted that AI companies are achieving super-linear growth by leveraging tools like coding agents and frontier models, scaling far faster than traditional SaaS businesses. This makes the billion-dollar exit an outdated benchmark for success, while open-source competition and data center bottlenecks are reshaping value distribution.

**Why it matters:** As AI companies reach economic scales in a fraction of the time it once took a decade to achieve, VC valuation anchors are shifting from "revenue multiples" to "compute efficiency" and "data moat density." This means early-stage investment criteria are moving from "team + product" to "infrastructure utilization + model iteration speed."
