Title: What to Watch Today Isn’t Just AI

There is a lot of information today, but the main thread is actually clear:  
AI is still expanding, but the market is starting to ask, “Can the money actually be earned back?”

1. The market is repricing Fed independence  
The U.S. Supreme Court temporarily blocked Trump from firing Federal Reserve Governor Lisa Cook.  
This is not just political news. It affects how markets judge the Fed’s independence. Going forward, traders may not only watch interest rates, but also “who can influence them.”

2. Chipmakers keep expanding aggressively  
Samsung and SK Hynix plan to invest massive sums in South Korea to build chip and AI infrastructure.  
The core signal: AI competition is no longer about whose model chats better, but who controls compute, storage, and manufacturing capacity.

3. AI money is flowing upstream  
AI service providers and application companies are burning cash, but the companies seeing stronger cash flow are memory and chip manufacturers.  
Put simply: many people are digging for gold, but the ones selling shovels make money first.

4. Microsoft’s plunge shows AI giants also need to deliver  
Microsoft’s market value has fallen by hundreds of billions of dollars in a month, as the market worries that AI spending is too high and returns are not coming fast enough.  
This shows the phase of “anything AI goes up” is over. Next, the focus will be revenue, margins, and product replacement risk.

5. AI bubble warnings are increasing  
The Bank for International Settlements warned that AI mania could threaten the global economy.  
Together with rising risks of “deleveraging events,” this suggests high-valuation assets are entering a stress test.

6. New energy is also being recalculated  
Spain has excess solar power capacity, pushing returns lower and causing investors to pull back.  
This is a reminder: even the best long-term trend can see returns crushed if short-term supply grows too fast.

7. Crypto assets are showing subtle changes  
Strategy said it may sell up to $1.25 billion worth of Bitcoin.  
If even long-term committed buyers start keeping room to sell, the market will reassess Bitcoin’s stability as corporate financing collateral.

8. AI safety and regulation are becoming a new battleground  
Zhipu’s model is considered close to Anthropic’s level in vulnerability detection, showing that cybersecurity is becoming a hard benchmark for model capability.  
At the same time, the U.S. partially easing restrictions on Anthropic models also shows AI export controls are becoming more refined.

9. AI is entering education, elections, and content production  
China is requiring schools at all levels to offer AI courses, U.S. AI funding is set to flow into the midterm elections, and the animation industry says AI can cut production costs by 90%.  
Taken together, these stories show AI is no longer just an industry. It is rewriting the cost structure of talent, politics, and creation.

Today’s one-line summary:  
AI remains the biggest theme, but the market has shifted from “imagination space” to “return verification.” What is truly worth watching now are the underlying variables: compute, power, chips, policy, and capital flows.
