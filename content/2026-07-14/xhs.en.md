# Today’s Must-Read: AI Adoption, the 2nm Race, Oil Prices, and Rate Hikes

If you follow only one main thread: rising Middle East risks are driving up oil prices and reshaping global interest-rate expectations. Meanwhile, AI competition is shifting from “who has the strongest model” to “who can truly embed AI in enterprises, smartphones, and infrastructure.”

## 1️⃣ The Middle East Becomes the Market’s Biggest Variable

The United States has struck Iran for a third consecutive night. Trump is still weighing whether to attack nuclear facilities and has reinstated a blockade of Iranian vessels. Attacks in the Strait of Hormuz are increasing, and there has even been a proposal to impose a 20% fee on related trade—though who would be charged and the legal basis remain unclear.

This affects far more than the conflict itself:

Oil prices have risen to a one-month high, and energy, aviation, and shipping costs may continue climbing. If higher energy prices feed into inflation, Federal Reserve policy will shift accordingly.

## 2️⃣ Markets Are Seriously Pricing In a Fed Rate Hike

Traders now put the probability of a July Fed rate hike at 50%. Fed officials have also warned that another hike cannot be ruled out if inflation remains persistently elevated.

The market debate has shifted from “when will rates be cut?” to “will rates be raised again?” In the near term, the dollar, short-term interest rates, and highly valued tech stocks are the key areas to watch. Meanwhile, the Fed is maintaining its reserve-management purchases at $10 billion, with liquidity operations yet to pivot as expected.

## 3️⃣ AI Competition Moves Into Enterprise Deployment and Smartphone Control

OpenAI’s GPT-5.6 Sol, Terra, and Luna are now fully available on Amazon Bedrock. This means frontier models can plug directly into enterprises’ existing security, governance, and procurement systems.

The contest will no longer be just about model rankings. Deployment speed, reliability on long-running tasks, and enterprise distribution channels will matter too.

In China, another path is emerging: StepFun has launched an AI agent smartphone that can use natural language to coordinate Alipay, Meituan, Didi, and Baidu to perform tasks across apps. The real tests are whether system permissions are sufficiently open, whether task success rates are high enough, and whether every action can be audited.

## 4️⃣ The 2nm Chip Race Starts Early

TSMC has reportedly begun mass-producing 2nm chips, and Google’s next smartphone may debut the technology before Apple. The next phase of smartphone-chip competition will be about more than peak performance—it will also hinge on power efficiency, yields, and capacity allocation.

Meanwhile, Samsung is preparing to manufacture Tesla’s AI5 chip at its Texas fab, also using a 2nm process. Tesla hopes to use a single in-house computing platform across FSD, Optimus, and AI data centers, but its success will depend heavily on Samsung’s yields, production ramp, and costs.

## 5️⃣ AI Infrastructure Enters the “Is There Enough Money?” Phase

U.S. data-center developers plan to sell tens of billions of dollars in equity, while RBC is expanding its credit-derivatives business to meet growing hedging demand tied to AI-related debt.

The signal is clear: demand for computing power remains strong, but the cost of capital is beginning to constrain expansion. Data-center valuations must now account for power, leases, financing costs, and real demand—not just the narrative of “AI scarcity.”

There are also signs that the memory-chip market is cooling. SK Hynix suffered its biggest one-day decline in 18 years, dragging down Micron and other chip stocks as investors reassess the memory cycle and whether the AI trade has become overcrowded.

## 6️⃣ Three More Global Economic Trends to Watch

China’s exports rose 27% year over year in June, widening the trade surplus to $125.6 billion as external demand significantly exceeded expectations.

In Europe, the EU failed to approve a new round of sanctions against Russia, leaving adjustments to the oil-price cap on hold. At the same time, the IMF warned that Europe’s debt trajectory could spiral out of control, with fiscal pressures once again influencing the pricing of European assets.

On the domestic policy front, the 2026 World Artificial Intelligence Conference will be held in Shanghai from July 17 to 20. New policy language on model safety, industry openness, cross-border governance, and foundation-model compliance may be worth watching for businesses.

Today’s keyword is not simply “AI.” It is this: geopolitical conflict is driving up energy prices, and energy prices are reshaping interest-rate expectations; meanwhile, AI is moving beyond the model race into a hard-fought battle over chips, access points, infrastructure, and financing.
