The key question now is whether higher energy prices will feed through into goods, transportation, and service prices. If that pass-through becomes pronounced, short-term interest rates, the dollar, and highly valued assets could remain under pressure. However, the Fed has kept its reserve-management purchases unchanged at $10 billion for now, suggesting that its liquidity operations have not yet shifted significantly in response to expectations of a rate hike.

B: Now let’s turn to AI. OpenAI’s GPT-5.6 Sol, Terra, and Luna are now fully available on Amazon Bedrock and integrated with its next-generation inference engine. The key point here is not simply that more new models have launched, but that frontier models are entering the primary channels through which enterprises procure and deploy AI.

A: Companies can continue using Bedrock’s existing permissions, security, and governance systems, reducing the time required for new procurement, compliance reviews, and infrastructure setup. As a result, model competition is shifting from one-off capability tests toward distribution access and reliability across long, complex workflows. Whether an agent can execute difficult tasks consistently may matter more than scoring a few points higher on a single response.

B: The battle for the agent entry point is also intensifying on the consumer side. StepFun has launched Step AOS, its personal agent Amoo, and the STEPX Neo smartphone. Users can complete tasks across different apps using natural language, with integrations currently available through Alipay, Meituan, Didi, and Baidu.

A: The real test for products like these is not how many apps they can call during a demonstration. It is whether system permissions are sensibly designed, whether task success rates are high enough, and whether every step can be tracked and undone. The ecosystem development over the next 100 days will help us determine whether system-level agents can become a new gateway to services.

B: There are also two developments on the chip front. First, TSMC’s 2-nanometer process has reportedly entered mass production, and Google may become its first smartphone-chip customer. Google’s new phone is expected to launch in mid-August, roughly one month ahead of Apple.

A: Being first to market could certainly strengthen the differentiation of Google’s Tensor chips, but the final outcome will still depend on power efficiency, yields, and capacity allocation. Meanwhile, Samsung is preparing to manufacture Tesla’s AI5 chip using its 2-nanometer process at its Taylor, Texas plant. The chip will support FSD, Optimus robots, and AI data centers, suggesting that Tesla wants to consolidate the computing needs of its different businesses onto an in-house platform.

B: However, the expansion of computing capacity is beginning to encounter capital constraints. U.S. data-center developers are planning to sell tens of billions of dollars in equity, while the Royal Bank of Canada is expanding its credit-derivatives business to meet demand for hedging debt linked to AI.

A: In other words, AI infrastructure is moving beyond the technology narrative and entering the balance-sheet phase. Investors will scrutinize power supplies, long-term leases, and financing costs more carefully. SK Hynix’s largest single-day decline in 18 years, which also dragged down chip stocks such as Micron, shows that the market is beginning to reassess the memory cycle and crowded valuations.

External demand significantly exceeded market expectations. The next question is whether this growth can continue and whether trade tensions will intensify as the surplus expands.

The divergence in U.S. and European policy timelines will continue to affect energy trade and the pricing of European assets.
