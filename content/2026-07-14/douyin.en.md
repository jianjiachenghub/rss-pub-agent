The biggest signal today isn’t another AI upgrade. It’s that the pricing logic for global assets is being rewritten—by energy conflict and high interest rates at the same time.

The U.S. has launched repeated strikes on Iran, raising shipping risks in the Strait of Hormuz and pushing oil prices to a one-month high. Markets have even priced in a 50% chance of a Fed rate hike in July. That means the dominant trade has suddenly shifted from “When will rates be cut?” to “Could rates rise again?” The dollar, highly valued tech stocks, airlines, and shipping companies will all face pressure.

But the AI race hasn’t stopped. It’s simply entered a more capital-intensive phase. OpenAI’s GPT-5.6 series is now fully available on Amazon Bedrock. The battle is no longer just about model rankings—it’s about becoming the gateway for enterprise deployment. Meanwhile, Tesla is preparing to have Samsung manufacture its AI5 chips using a 2-nanometer process, unifying computing power for vehicles, robots, and data centers on its in-house platform.

The problem is, scaling compute is getting more expensive. Data centers are increasingly selling equity stakes, while AI-related debt is already driving demand for credit hedging.

In one sentence: AI is still accelerating—but oil prices, interest rates, and financing costs are now deciding who can actually stay in the race to the end.
