Today's Assessment: The cost of the AI arms race is soaring, while regulatory and safety concerns are becoming prominent industry constraints.

1. AI infrastructure investment has hit new highs, with U.S. tech giants spending over $700 billion this year, indicating the capital competition has entered an intense phase.
2. Regulatory restrictions are tightening. The White House opposes the expanded commercial use of high-risk models like Anthropic's Mythos, and Goldman Sachs has also banned employee use of Claude. Data security has become a critical threshold for AI commercialization.
3. AI costs are rising significantly. The price of Anthropic's coding assistant has doubled to $13 per day, forcing businesses to reassess the input-output ratio of AI tools.

What to Watch Next: How enterprise-level AI applications can demonstrate their commercial value under the dual pressures of compliance and cost.
