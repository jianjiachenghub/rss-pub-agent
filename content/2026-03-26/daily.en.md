OpenAI tightening the Sora subscription gateway is an extremely important signal: **AI commercialization is bowing to real-world costs**. When Jensen Huang introduced the **"Token Factory"** concept, it meant compute is transitioning from a high-tech scarce resource to standardized **infrastructure**.

### Why it matters
This means the threshold logic for large model startups has changed. Previously, the competition was about model capability; now it's about **"cost per kilowatt-hour."** As Tokens become commoditized, model vendors' profit margins will be compressed, similar to the price wars about to unfold in the cloud computing market.

### What should developers and entrepreneurs do?
1.  **Rebuild cost models**: Don't assume compute costs will decline indefinitely. When designing application architecture, **Token cost** must be a core KPI.
2.  **Avoid pure model-layer investments**: If you're an investor, be wary of companies without closed-loop scenarios that rely solely on selling Tokens—they're easily crushed by the scale effects of giants.
