**Podcast Script: Personal Daily**

**Duration:** Approx. 4 minutes 50 seconds  
**Roles:** Host A (Macro Perspective), Host B (Industry Perspective)

---

**A:** The market narrative today is actually quite clear—investment banks are repricing risk assets through the AI storyline. Goldman Sachs just raised its year-end S&P 500 target to 8,000, implying 17% upside from here.

**B:** Right, and the reasoning is specific: AI-driven corporate earnings growth. This means Wall Street has determined that AI is no longer conceptual hype, but will actually show up in the financial statements. When major banks systematically upgrade earnings expectations for tech stocks, the market’s valuation anchor shifts up accordingly, and subsequent capital flows and sector allocation weights will adjust.

**A:** But on the other hand, geopolitical risks haven’t disappeared. Middle East tensions should theoretically be boosting gold, but gold has actually fallen for two consecutive days.

**B:** The key here is that the market is now more worried the conflict will prolong inflation, forcing the Fed to maintain high interest rates. Gold, as a zero-yield asset, sees its carrying costs rise under hiking expectations. This shows capital is currently more focused on the Fed’s policy trajectory than on pure safe-haven demand.

**A:** Returning to AI itself, capital is indeed pouring in aggressively. ByteDance was reported to be spending $70 billion on AI this year—more than double last year—mainly for building data centers and procuring compute chips.

**B:** Capital expenditure of this magnitude directly pulls up order expectations for suppliers like NVIDIA. The AI arms race among Chinese tech giants has moved from the R&D phase into a capital-intensive heavy investment phase. Moreover, ByteDance is also doubling down at the organizational level, issuing "Doubao shares" to Seed department employees at $13 per share, plus additional options, with monthly option grants worth up to RMB 130,000 per person.

**A:** These are targeted incentives for AI talent. TSMC is doing something similar—due to surging profits from AI chip business, the CEO promised employee bonuses would rise by an average of more than 30% this year. This repricing of labor costs shows the entire industry is locking in key technical talent.

**B:** However, not all Chinese AI companies are having smooth sailing. When DeepSeek released its V4 model last month, the market reaction was quite muted, failing to recreate the splash it made with R1 earlier this year. This suggests the iteration cycle for foundation models is lengthening, and single technical breakthroughs can no longer sustain market excitement indefinitely.

**A:** But the hardware side is genuinely benefiting. Both SK Hynix and Micron saw their market caps break $100 billion for the first time. High Bandwidth Memory (HBM) has become the bottleneck link in AI infrastructure. The Nordic data center company PolarDC even dared to issue €800 million in junk bonds—a regional record—showing capital is willing to accept higher risks for AI infrastructure.

**B:** The primary market is indeed scorching hot. AI coding company Cognition is valued at $26 billion, and SpaceX’s IPO documents have leaked, possibly listing by mid-June. The ECB specifically issued a warning today, saying investors are underestimating geopolitical risks and the market faces the possibility of sudden sharp corrections. This signal of tightening regulatory scrutiny is worth noting.

**A:** Regulation is tightening, but the application layer is accelerating. Robinhood now lets users buy stocks directly using AI agents, even supporting credit card payments—effectively lowering both the trading barrier and the leverage barrier. Meanwhile, OpenAI is also hiring safety experts at high salaries to address the risks of AI self-evolution.

**B:** On one side there’s aggressive expansion, on the other side they’re starting to consider safety brakes. What we need to watch next is who adjusts first—the 8,000-point optimistic expectations or the real risks.

**A:** That’s the situation today. AI is reshaping every pricing link from the secondary market to the primary market.

**B:** Right, but interest rates and geopolitical risks remain variables hanging overhead. See you tomorrow.
