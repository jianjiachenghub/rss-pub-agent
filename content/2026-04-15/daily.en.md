### 2026 Stanford AI Index Report: US AI Investment 23x China's, But AI Model Gap Virtually Closed

**Event:** Stanford University's Institute for Human-Centered Artificial Intelligence released the "2026 AI Index Report," noting unprecedented AI technology adoption with generative AI penetration at 53%, enterprise adoption at 88%, and over 80% of university students using it. Globally, AI industry leadership in model R&D shows the US-China AI technology gap has been virtually erased—China leads in papers, patents, and industrial robot installations, while the US leads in top-tier models, high-value patents, and investment. In computing power, global AI compute is growing rapidly, with NVIDIA holding over 60% market share. However, AI development also brings high power and water consumption issues. Key report points include: US and China are evenly matched in model technology, with competition focusing on costs and other factors. AI model development is rapid, but benchmark error rates remain high, with issues including insufficient transparency and high hallucination rates. AI is beginning to impact employment, though its effects are difficult to measure. People are both optimistic and anxious about AI, with divergence between expert and public views. Governments worldwide are working to regulate AI, but regulation still lags behind technological development.
