**【Entry 1: Alibaba Upgrades Large Model Organizational Structure】**

**A:** Alibaba merged Tongyi large models and the Future Life Laboratory to establish the Token Foundry division, with Eddie Wu personally taking charge. Notice the name—Token Foundry. Literally translated, it means "token minting factory."

**B:** That's a pretty hardcore name.

**A:** Also, Zhou Jingren is specifically heading up the AI Future Research Institute, and original business lines like Happy Horse have been merged into it. What does this tell us? Alibaba has elevated large models from independent business units to the strategic core, shortening the decision-making chain directly to the CEO.

**B:** In other words, how traffic gets distributed across Alibaba's ecosystem will probably depend on model capabilities going forward, rather than individual business lines calling the shots.

**A:** Exactly. R&D resources are concentrating on frontier exploration and foundation models. They're preparing for a long war.

---

**【Entry 2: ChatGPT Redesign】**

**B:** Speaking of long-term battles, OpenAI is making even bolder moves. They just hit a billion monthly active users, and now they're turning around to change that familiar chat interface.

**A:** Right. The signal is clear. ChatGPT is no longer just "a chatbot." It's becoming an OS-level entry point. That chat box has carried too many functions, and now it may need to be decoupled into more underlying components.

**B:** Users might be uncomfortable at first, but from a business perspective, OpenAI is breaking free from the limitation of "conversation as product" and moving toward platformization. Plainly speaking, it's erasing the "chat" label because future AI goes beyond chatting.

---

**【Entry 3: Apple's Budget AI Strategy】**

**A:** The flip side of platformization is ecosystem competition. Apple dropped a bombshell at WWDC targeting small developers: if your downloads are under 2 million, you can use Foundation Models' cloud capabilities for free.

**B:** That's pretty clever. Big developers aren't short on cash, but independent developers care most about costs. Apple is using "budget AI" or even "free AI" to buy ecosystem loyalty. This is competing with OpenAI and Google for developer resources.

**A:** Plus they emphasize Private Cloud, which addresses privacy concerns while lowering barriers. This "soft penetration" is way more effective than aggressively pushing APIs. It's basically telling independent developers: use it for free first, and once you've built good apps, then we'll talk about revenue sharing.

---

**【Entry 4: MIIT Cracks Down on APP Redirects】**

**B:** Looking domestically, the MIIT has recently been cracking down on APP redirects. While this might not sound highly technical, it affects everyone's daily experience.

**A:** Exactly. Those shake-to-advertise features, automatic redirects—users have suffered from them for ages. Now regulators are explicitly requiring standardized information window displays and strictly prohibiting non-compliant redirects. This is finally cutting away at the "psoriasis" of the mobile internet.

**B:** And this time they're guiding and supervising both platforms and device manufacturers to regulate from both sides—managing both the APPs themselves and system-level interception on phones. For average users, things might finally get a bit quieter.
