# Personal Daily | June 12, 2026

## Today's Take

> AI capability parity is accelerating. The price war triggered by Chinese models is not merely a cost competition, but a harbinger of a global shift in AI supply chain power—when Kimi enters with an open-source, 6× speed posture, OpenAI and Anthropic are forced to confront eroding pricing power. Meanwhile, the emergency brake on the SPCX IPO and the conversion of Pre-IPO contracts signal that capital markets' liquidity prudence toward high-valuation hard tech has reached a critical point. The dual squeeze of model commoditization and capital risk repricing will reshape industry sequencing over the next three months.

### Anthropic CEO: Governments Should Have Authority to Block High-Risk New Model Deployments

**Event:** Anthropic CEO Dario Amodei stated that if new AI models pose specific risks, governments should have the authority to block their deployment. He advocates that AI models undergo mandatory third-party testing to assess risks across multiple domains, arguing that the appropriate analogy for AI at its current stage is automobiles, aircraft, or pharmaceuticals—technologies vital to the modern economy but potentially capable of causing mass casualties if poorly designed or misused. Anthropic had previously warned that its Mythos AI model could identify and exploit critical software vulnerabilities, deciding to open it only to select partners. This week, it released a new version with those offensive cyber capabilities removed.

**Why it matters:** A top AI CEO's explicit policy stance signals the future regulatory path for high-risk models, shaping industry compliance expectations.

### SpaceX Completes Record-Breaking $75 Billion IPO, Musk's Net Worth Nears $1 Trillion

**Event:** SpaceX priced its IPO at $135 per share, completing a $75 billion offering at a $1.77 trillion valuation—surpassing Saudi Aramco's 2019 record to become the largest IPO in history. **Why it matters:** SpaceX's $1.77 trillion valuation establishes a pricing benchmark for private aerospace and defense-tech assets. ## Watch Signals

Three key variables demand close attention going forward: First, whether the AI model cost curve continues to steepen, which will determine the inflection point for application-layer growth. Second, the liquidity linkage between Pre-IPO and crypto derivatives markets, to observe whether risk capital is materially retreating from hard tech. Third, the speed at which regulatory rhetoric translates into concrete policy, particularly the pace of establishing high-risk model review mechanisms. The direction of their combined resonance will determine the tightness of the technology-capital cycle in the second half of the year.
