AI compute is running short—and being repriced by the market at the same time.

The key takeaway today: AI competition has shifted from “who has the best model” to “who can reliably deliver more inference compute at a lower cost.”

China’s daily token usage has surged from around 100 billion to 140 trillion in just over two years—a more than thousandfold increase. Every task completed by an AI agent may involve dozens of model calls, naturally multiplying compute demand.

The clearest signal comes from Kimi: just 48 hours after launching its new model, it paused new consumer subscriptions due to compute shortages. Meanwhile, Meta may even lease up to $10 billion worth of compute capacity to Anthropic. In the future, idle compute could flow between companies like cloud services do today.

But capital markets are already hitting the brakes. The AI trade, previously up 105%, has cooled sharply, while chip stocks have fallen into a technical bear market. Investors are no longer asking only how much is being spent. They want to know: when will all this compute start generating revenue?

So what’s really worth watching next isn’t just the model rankings. It’s cost per token, chip utilization—and who can turn compute into cash flow the fastest.
