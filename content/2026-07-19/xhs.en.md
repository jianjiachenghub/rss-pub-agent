Title: Today’s Must-Read Stories Go Beyond AI

July 19 was packed with news. If I had to focus on the main threads, I’d watch these five developments:

1️⃣ AI is becoming a “compute business”

China’s average daily token usage has grown more than a thousandfold in just over two years. Kimi has even paused new user subscriptions due to a shortage of computing capacity, while Meta may lease up to $10 billion worth of compute to Anthropic.

This shows that the industry’s central question is shifting from “Do the models work?” to “Is there enough compute, how fast can it be allocated, and how much does each token cost?”

2️⃣ AI phones and robots are entering the race for real-world adoption

The second-generation “Doubao phone” can carry out tasks across apps, potentially shifting the smartphone experience from “opening apps yourself” to “simply assigning a task.”

Meanwhile, around 60 full-size humanoid robots were deployed at WAIC. Going forward, don’t just look at whether robots can walk or dance—look at how long they can operate continuously, how often they fail, and how much each task costs.

3️⃣ The AI stock frenzy is cooling

Chip stocks have fallen into a technical bear market, SpaceX has lost more than $1 trillion in value from its peak, and retail investors making leveraged bets on AI have suffered significant losses.

The market is starting to ask a more practical question: After all this investment in compute, when will the revenue and cash flow materialize? The margin for error on highly valued tech assets is shrinking.

4️⃣ Geopolitical conflict is raising energy and inflation risks

A third U.S. service member has been killed in the U.S.-Iran conflict, while Russia has launched a large-scale barrage of ballistic missiles at Kyiv. At the same time, hedge funds are increasing bullish oil positions at the fastest pace in a decade.

Conflict, oil prices, and inflation are feeding into one another, making the European Central Bank’s interest-rate path even harder to predict.

5️⃣ Risk appetite is diverging across financial markets

On one side, retail investors are piling into high-risk crypto products. On the other, U.S. crypto legislation may be entering a window for progress, while Singapore is considering tax incentives to compete with Hong Kong for hedge funds and talent.

Today’s keywords are not simply “the AI boom,” but compute shortages, valuation resets, escalating geopolitical tensions, bullish oil bets, and competition between financial hubs.

In one sentence: Technology is still accelerating, but capital is starting to scrutinize the numbers—and global risks are rising at the same time. 🌍
